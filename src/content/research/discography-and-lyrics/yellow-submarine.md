# Yellow Submarine (1969)

## Recording Context
* **Location:** EMI Studios (Abbey Road) and De Lane Lea Studios, London.
* **The Project:** A contractual obligation to provide new music for the United Artists animated film. The band was initially uninterested in the project, but they were so impressed by the film's innovative animation that they eventually contributed more material.
* **Spiritual/Individual State:**
    * **The Collective:** The album serves as a bridge between the peak psychedelia of 1967 and the more rock-oriented 1968. It is unique for featuring two significant George Harrison compositions on a single-disc release.
    * **John Lennon:** Provided the "nursery-rhyme" style rock of "Hey Bulldog," showcasing a rare moment of lighthearted band collaboration during the tense early '68 period.
    * **George Harrison:** Exploring cynicism toward the music industry ("Only A Northern Song") and profound philosophical transcendence ("It's All Too Much").
    * **George Martin:** Side Two of the original LP featured his orchestral score for the film, emphasizing his vital role in the "Beatles sound."
