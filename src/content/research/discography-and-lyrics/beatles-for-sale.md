# Beatles for Sale (1964)

## Recording Context
* **Location:** EMI Studios (Abbey Road), London.
* **The Fatigue Factor:** Recorded during the height of 1964's global hysteria. The band was physically and mentally exhausted, which is visible on the album cover. To meet the Christmas market deadline, they returned to recording covers (6 out of 14 tracks) for the last time until the *Let It Be* sessions.
* **Spiritual/Individual State:**
    * **The "Tired" Collective:** The manic energy of *A Hard Day's Night* has been replaced by a more somber, autumn-hued reflection.
    * **John Lennon:** Deeply influenced by meeting Bob Dylan in August 1964. His lyrics become more autobiographical and "confessional" (e.g., "I'm a Loser").
    * **Paul McCartney:** Balancing John's darkness with his own "work-song" optimism, though even his contributions (like "I'll Follow the Sun") have a bittersweet, nostalgic quality.
    * **George Harrison:** Continuing to refine his rockabilly influence (Carl Perkins) while leaning into the folk-rock 12-string sound.
    * **Ringo Starr:** Given the spotlight on "Honey Don't," showcasing his affinity for country and rockabilly.
