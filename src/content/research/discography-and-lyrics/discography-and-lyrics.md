# The Beatles: Master Discography & Context

This document tracks the physical recording locations and the spiritual/individual movement of the band members. Detailed lyrics for each album can be found in the linked files below.

---

## 1. Please Please Me (1963)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios (Abbey Road), Studio 2, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Fresh from the Star-Club in Hamburg; captured in a 585-minute marathon session.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Total "gang" unity. They are driven by a singular, secular ambition to escape their working-class roots. John is the undisputed leader; Paul is the eager lieutenant.
* **Tracklist & Credits:** * *I Saw Her Standing There* (McCartney/Lennon), *Please Please Me* (Lennon/McCartney), *Twist and Shout* (Medley/Russell), etc.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Please Please Me](./please-please-me)

---

## 2. With The Beatles (1963)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Recorded in short bursts between a relentless touring schedule.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** The beginning of the "mop-top" mythos. George begins to feel the first stirrings of individual frustration ("Don't Bother Me"), seeking his own space within the Lennon/McCartney powerhouse.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for With The Beatles](./with-the-beatles)

---

## 3. A Hard Day's Night (1964)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London; Pathé Marconi, Paris (singles).
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** The height of Beatlemania; filming their first movie simultaneously.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** A period of John’s peak creative dominance. The band is a "closed circle," spiritually insulated from the outside world by their shared, unique fame.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for A Hard Day's Night](./a-hard-days-night)

---

## 4. Beatles For Sale (1964)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Extreme physical and mental exhaustion.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** A shift toward melancholy. Influenced by Bob Dylan, John begins moving toward internal, confessional songwriting ("I'm a Loser"), signaling a departure from standard pop optimism.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Beatles For Sale](./beatles-for-sale)

---

## 5. Help! (1965)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London; locations in The Bahamas and Austria.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** The "marijuana period" begins in earnest.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** John is in his "Fat Elvis" period—feeling spiritually trapped by domesticity and the "Help!" he cries for is literal. Paul is expanding into sophisticated arrangement ("Yesterday").
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Help!](./help)

---

## 6. Rubber Soul (1965)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** A focused, month-long studio "lock-in."
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** The "Coming of Age." George introduces the sitar, marking the first move toward Eastern spirituality. They are collectively moving away from "the show" and toward "the art."
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Rubber Soul](./rubber-soul)

---

## 7. Revolver (1966)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Total studio immersion; they decide to stop touring.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Exploration of ego-death via LSD. John is reading *The Tibetan Book of the Dead*; George is studying with Ravi Shankar. They are spiritually unified by a desire to dismantle their own pop personas.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Revolver](./revolver)

---

## 8. Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios; Regent Sound, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** 700+ hours of recording.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Paul takes the creative lead. George is spiritually detached, feeling the project is trivial. John is in a psychedelic haze, focusing on surreal internal landscapes.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Sgt. Pepper](./sgt-pepper)

---

## 9. Magical Mystery Tour (1967)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios; Olympic Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Disorganized filming on the road in England.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Grief-stricken and rudderless following Brian Epstein’s death. They are searching for a new direction, leading to a fragmented, "dream-logic" spiritual state.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Magical Mystery Tour](./magical-mystery-tour)

---

## 10. The Beatles (The White Album) (1968)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios; Trident Studios, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Following the retreat to Rishikesh, India.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** The "Great Fragmentation." They are no longer a group but four individuals. John is spiritually reborn through Yoko Ono; George is finding independence; Paul is desperately trying to be the "manager."
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for The White Album](./the-white-album)

---

## 11. Yellow Submarine (1969)
* **Recording Location:** Various (1967-1968 leftovers).
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Minimal collective involvement.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Indifference. George uses the sessions to express frustration with the band's business structure ("Only a Northern Song").
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Yellow Submarine](./yellow-submarine)

---

## 12. Abbey Road (1969)
* **Recording Location:** EMI Studios; Olympic; Trident, London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** A "professional truce" to record one last masterpiece.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Acceptance of the end. Paul crafts the "Medley" as a goodbye; George finally achieves songwriting parity. There is a sense of "coming home" before the final departure.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Abbey Road](./abbey-road)

---

## 13. Let It Be (1970)
* **Recording Location:** Twickenham Film Studios; Apple Studios (Savile Row), London.
* **Band State:** * **Physical:** Cold, early-morning sessions under the glare of film cameras.
    * **Spiritual/Individual:** Total collapse. John is mentally already solo; George is resentful of Paul's "direction"; Paul is terrified of the band’s dissolution. The Rooftop Concert is their final, brief spiritual reunion.
* **Lyrics:** [View Full Lyrics for Let It Be](./let-it-be)
