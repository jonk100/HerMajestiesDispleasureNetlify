# Let It Be (1970)

## Recording Context
* **Location:** Twickenham Film Studios and Apple Studio (Savile Row), London.
* **The Project:** Conceived as a televised rehearsal and concert showing the band "getting back" to their rock and roll roots. The project was plagued by interpersonal tension and was shelved for over a year before being produced by Phil Spector.
* **Spiritual/Individual State:**
    * **The Collective:** The group was on the verge of collapse. George Harrison briefly quit the band during the Twickenham rehearsals. Despite the friction, the introduction of keyboardist Billy Preston helped stabilize the group for the final Apple Studio sessions.
    * **John Lennon:** Often distracted and accompanied by Yoko Ono, yet still capable of profound beauty ("Across the Universe") and raw rock energy ("I've Got a Feeling").
    * **Paul McCartney:** The primary driver of the project, attempting to keep the band unified through anthems like "Let It Be" and "The Long and Winding Road."
    * **George Harrison:** Increasingly frustrated with his role, he contributed the bluesy "For You Blue" and the biting "I Me Mine," which addressed the egos within the band.
