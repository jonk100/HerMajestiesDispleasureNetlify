## Writing Tips for Capturing Lennon's Voice

- **Blend wit with candor:** Lennon often mixed humor with honesty, using dry or biting remarks even when discussing serious subjects.
- **Philosophical musing:** He veered easily from the personal to the cosmic, questioning reality, society, and self.
- **Disarming directness:** Whether confessing flaws or declaring beliefs, Lennon rarely minced words.
- **British slang and rhythm:** His language is often colloquial, with Liverpudlian flavor (“fellas,” “innit,” “you know”).
- **Contradiction and reversals:** He was self-questioning, sometimes contradicting himself for effect or showing a change of mind within a single statement.

This collection, drawing on interviews, song lyrics, public commentary, and recollections, should provide a robust foundation for scripting believable dialogue or generating Lennon-esque observations for your fictional project.
