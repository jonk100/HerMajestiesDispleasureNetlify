## Day 2 Overview (January 3, 1969)

Second day of rehearsals at Twickenham Film Studios. The Beatles reconvened on January 6 following the weekend break.

### Early Morning Session (Paul & Ringo arrive first)

**Paul's piano work on unfinished tracks:**
- "The Long And Winding Road" (later on Let It Be album)
- "Oh! Darling" (later on Abbey Road album)
- "Maxwell's Silver Hammer" (later remade for Abbey Road)
- "Let It Be" (later on Let It Be album)

**Original compositions introduced:**
- Ringo: "Taking A Trip To Carolina"
- Ringo: "Picasso"
- George: "Ramblin' Woman" (Dylanesque piece)

### Full Band Session (John arrives)

**Rock 'n' roll classics and early Lennon-McCartney material:**
- "Won't You Please Say Goodbye"
- "Thinking Of Linking"
- "I'll Wait Till Tomorrow"
- "Because I Know You Love Me So"
- "One After 909"

### Main Rehearsals (Newer Material)

#### Don't Let Me Down
- 10 versions rehearsed
- Final arrangement nearly complete
- **John's concern:** "It hurts just to rehearse it" (regarding vocal delivery)
- **Length timing:** John asks Mal Evans to time it with stopwatch
- **Result:** 3 minutes 40 seconds ("That's fine then")
- **Notable moment:** John inserted segment of "Happiness Is A Warm Gun" as humorous ad lib
- **Next session:** Continued on January 6

#### I've Got A Feeling
- 6 run-throughs to tighten performance
- **Paul's concern:** Bridge section weakness ("All these years I've been wandering around")
- **Harmony attempts:** Paul suggests John harmonize to support his high register
- **John's response:** "That's very high for me...It's a bit early for that. Early in the morning, y'know. I'm not 18 anymore!"
- **Resolution:** John's harmony dropped in favor of Paul's solo vocal
- **Next session:** Continued on January 6

#### Two Of Us
- Few run-throughs before lunch
- **John's comment:** "Lovely tune"
- **Paul's note:** Glyn Johns suggested doing it on two acoustics
- **Next session:** Continued on January 6

#### Sun King
- 4 versions recorded
- **Next session:** One quick version on January 10, then shelved temporarily

### New Material Introduced

#### Gimme Some Truth (John)
- First introduction to the band
- Work-in-progress status
- **Paul's contribution:** "Money for rope" section (Paul had no memory of this later)
- **Peter Jackson revelation:** Paul co-wrote portions but forgot by 2021
- **Paul's reaction:** "It's a great song. I've always loved that song so if I get some credit for it, great"
- **Beatles outcome:** Left aside during sessions
- **Future release:** John's solo album "Imagine" (1971)
- **Next session:** Returned to on January 7

#### All Things Must Pass (George)
- 37 attempts seeking workable arrangement
- Previous day: George performed solo
- This day: Full band rehearsal (John on organ, Paul on bass, Ringo on drums)
- **Notable incident:** George received electric shock from microphone
- **Paul's reaction:** "Gonna be in trouble over this. If this boy dies, you're gonna cop it"
- **Next sessions:** January 6, January 8, January 28, January 29

### End of Day Session

#### Maxwell's Silver Hammer
- 10 rehearsals total
- **Status:** Only first verse, chorus, and half of second verse written
- **Paul's description:** "The corny one"
- **Arrangement attempts:** 
  - Paul on bass calling out chords for John and George
  - Switched to piano at George's suggestion
  - George on Fender Bass VI
- **Film appearance:** Small segment in released "Let It Be" movie
- **Paul's request:** Bring in hammer and anvil (fulfilled by Mal Evans on January 7)
- **Ultimate fate:** Remade for Abbey Road album
- **Next session:** January 7

### Chronological Conversations

#### Conversation 1: Live Show Planning - Setlist Discussion

**Context:** Discussion about whether to include older Beatles songs in the live show or perform only new material.

**George Harrison:** "Are we not going to do any 'oldies but goldies' on this show?"

**Paul McCartney:** [Uncertain, but open to it]

**George Harrison:** [Explains marketing perspective - audience needs something to identify with if playing all new songs; suggests starting or ending show with familiar material]

**John Lennon:** "Help!" done Joe Cocker-style - "I've been doing 'Help!' pretty good"

**Yoko Ono:** [Endorses "Help!" as nice choice]

**George Harrison and Paul McCartney:** [Played "Every Little Girl"]

#### Conversation 2: Career & Album Planning - Band Chemistry & Future Direction

**Context:** George compares Bonnie & Delaney band to Beatles' current state - "That band...are more together than we've ever been. You know, as a band"

**John Lennon:** "If we get into the swinging of playing, let's do an album after this album. You know, about a month later"

**John Lennon:** [Suggests avoiding previous pattern of long breaks affecting band chemistry; get to "a good peak playing, and then we split" - ambiguous phrasing]

**Paul McCartney:** "We should...organise our career, man"

**Paul McCartney:** "This is like the idea of this. It's to get us so we quite enjoy this"

**George Harrison:** "We've got to get through that bit of shit of the meeting until we get it together again"

**George Harrison:** [Explains hard work to remember all words, get guitar parts right, perform when not ready]

**George Harrison:** "I mean, we've got to do that in order to get the good bit"

**George Harrison:** "There's so much to get out, you know"

#### Conversation 3: Billy Preston Discussion - Ray Charles' Band

**Context:** George's enthusiasm for Ray Charles' band and Billy Preston. Beatles first met Preston in 1962 (Little Richard's touring band). Foreshadows Preston joining Get Back sessions later when they move to Apple Studios.

**George Harrison:** "The best jazz band I saw was Ray Charles' band"

**George Harrison:** "That jazz really moves me in a fantastic way"

**George Harrison:** "Billy Preston is too much. I'm sure you'll dig it when you see"

**George Harrison:** [Explains Preston plays piano with band, does solo spot singing/dancing/playing organ]

**George Harrison:** "He's better than Ray Charles, really, because he's, like, too much!"

**George Harrison:** "He plays organ so great now"

**George Harrison:** [Explains Ray Charles delegates organ to Billy] "I'll leave it to the young guy, Billy"
