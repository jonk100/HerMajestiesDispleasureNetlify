# MI5 Q-Branch (Quartermaster Stores)

The heart of MI5's logistical operation, but a heart that stopped beating sometime around 1958. This is where Nigel and Clive come for their equipment and instead receive a lecture from Percy Dalton on the importance of proper paperwork and the folly of youth.

## Visual Description

A cathedral of obsolescence. The Q-Branch is a vast, chilly basement storeroom filled with floor-to-ceiling metal shelves. Every box and item has a neatly typed label, but everything is coated in a uniform layer of fine grey dust. This is a graveyard for ambitious technology: Bakelite phones, valve-based transmitters that hum audibly, and cameras that require a shoulder strap and a strong back.

The centerpiece is Percy's counter, a long, imposing wooden barrier that separates him from the "field agents" he so deeply distrusts. On it are his tools: meticulously organized forms, rubber stamps for every conceivable purpose, and a well-used magnifying glass for inspecting returned "assets" for microscopic damage.

## Atmosphere

## Symbolic Function

Q-Branch is a physical manifestation of the entire conspiracy's core problem: a grand, aristocratic ambition being executed with inadequate, second-rate tools by a dysfunctional bureaucracy. It symbolizes the disconnect between the old guard (Mountbatten, Philip), who imagine a world of sleek efficiency, and the mundane reality of the civil service they command. It's a place where the thrilling fantasy of espionage dies and the boring reality of inventory management reigns supreme.
