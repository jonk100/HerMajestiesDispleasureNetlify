# Scene Outline: Get Back Sessions (Replacing S19)

Based on the research, proposing **5-7 scenes** that capture the authentic drama of the Get Back sessions, showing both the downfall AND the moments of hope. This gives a more accurate and dramatically rich portrayal than a single scene of dissolution.

---

## **SCENE 19A: "I'm Scared of Being the Boss"**
**Location:** Twickenham Film Studios  
**Day:** January 6, 1969 (Day 3)  
**Page Count:** 2-3 pages

### Purpose
Establish the creative tensions and Paul's awareness of his own controlling behavior. Shows Operation Minuet's effects amplifying existing problems.

### Key Beats
- Beatles rehearsing "Two Of Us" - not coming together
- Paul trying to direct, getting frustrated
- **George pushing back:** "It's not complicated. I'll play just the chords, if you like"
- **Paul's vulnerable confession:** "I'm scared of me being the boss. And I have been for a couple of years. I always feel as though I'm trying to put you down and stop you playing. But I'm not. I'm trying to stop us all playing, until we know what we're playing"
- **Paul continues:** "I never get any support, or anything. So I just think, 'Oh, well. Fuck it'. And I know it's right, and you know it's right"
- **George (resigned):** "I'll wait until you get all your bits and then work my part out, if you like"
- Ends with Paul saying "Let's do another song" and the tension unresolved

### Story Arc Support
- Shows Paul's self-awareness (important for later Paul arc)
- George's frustration building (leads to his departure)
- John mostly silent (his detachment growing)

## **SCENE 19B: "Daddy's Gone Away"**
**Location:** Twickenham Film Studios  
**Day:** January 7, 1969 (Day 4)  
**Page Count:** 3-4 pages

### Purpose
Peak of tensions before George's departure. Show the existential crisis about the band's future and purpose.

### Key Beats
- Morning discussion about live show venue (absurd suggestions - orphanage, hospital)
- **Paul's desperate question:** "We gotta have some serious reason for doing this or else we won't. What's it for? Can't be for the money"
- **John:** "I've decided the whole point of it is communication"
- **Paul on Brian Epstein:** "Ever since Mr. Epstein passed away, it's never been the same... Daddy's gone away now, and we're on our own at the holiday camp. I think we either go home or we do it"
- **George:** "The Beatles have been in doldrums for at least a year"
- **The divorce joke:**
  - **George:** "Maybe we should have a divorce"
  - **Paul:** "Well, I said that at the last meeting. But it's getting nearer, you know?"
  - **John:** "Who'd have the children?"
  - **Paul:** "Dick James"

## **SCENE 19D: "The Lunch Conversation"**  
**Location:** Twickenham Film Studios - Cafeteria  
**Day:** January 13, 1969 (Day 8)  
**Page Count:** 3-4 pages

### Purpose
The secret conversation revealing the deep creative issues. Shows John and Paul's complexity - they understand the problems but can't solve them.

### Key Beats
- Paul and John in cafeteria, unaware of hidden microphone
- **John on George:** "It's like George said, he didn't get enough satisfaction anymore, because of the compromise he had to make to be together. It's a festering wound that we've allowed to... and yesterday we allowed it to go even deeper, and we didn't give him any bandages. And when he is that far in, we have egos"
- **Paul:** "The thing is, that's what I was trying to say to George, you know. Whereas previously I would have said, 'Take it there with diddle-der-diddle-der'. But I was trying last week to say, 'Now take it there. Anything you like. Put whatever you...'"
- **John:** "You see, the point is now, we both do that to George this time, and because of the build-up to it"
- **Paul:** "Yeah, we treat him a bit like that. See, 'cause he knows what we're on about"
- Discussion of creative process - John can't "see" arrangements like Paul can
- **John:** "There was a period where none of us could actually say anything about your arrangements 'cause you would reject it all"

## **SCENE 19E: "Billy Preston Arrives"**
**Location:** Apple Studios Basement  
**Day:** January 22, 1969 (Day 13)  
**Page Count:** 2-3 pages

### Purpose
THE TURNAROUND. Show that genuine joy and creativity are still possible. This is crucial - not just dissolution, but hope.

### Key Beats
- Apple Studios - new environment, smaller, warmer
- Beatles working, but George still present (tentative)
- **Billy Preston arrives** - George's invitation
- **John to Billy:** "Every number's got a piano part. And normally we overdub it. But this time we wanna do it live"
- Billy sits at electric piano
- They start "I've Got A Feeling"
- **Magic happens** - the sound comes alive
- **John's immediate reaction:** "You're in the group!"
- Everyone beaming, smiles all around
- **George (to camera/Michael):** "Having this fifth person was just enough to cut the ice that we'd created among ourselves. Billy didn't know all the politics and the games that had been going on, so in his innocence he got stuck in and gave an extra little kick to the band"

### Quote Integration
Use Billy Preston arrival dialogue and George's retrospective quote (from day-13-overview.md:62-89 and day-13-overview.md:189-221)

### Story Arc Support
- Shows the band CAN still function
- George's contribution (bringing Billy) saves the sessions
- Sets up the creative renewal that leads to rooftop concert

## **SCENE 19G: "The Rooftop"**
**Location:** Apple Studios - Rooftop Discussion  
**Day:** January 25, 1969 (Day 16)  
**Page Count:** 2-3 pages

### Purpose
The solution. Show Paul's frustration transforming into hope. The rooftop concert as salvation.

### Key Beats
- Control room - listening to playbacks
- **Paul frustrated:** "I just have a feeling that we... It's all right, you know? It's nothing to complain about, but... But it's just funny to sort of realize that after this is all over, you'll be off in a black bag somewhere on the Albert Hall, you know?"
- **Michael Lindsay-Hogg:** "What you're moaning about is there's no payoff"
- **Paul:** "I'd like to sort of do a... for the finish. And we just get out in the open, change of scene, and go and do it somewhere else, do it on a live show, do it on a stage. You know, I'd like to light a rocket and really sort of take off for the end of it"
- Someone suggests checking out the roof
- They go up - Paul, Ringo, Glyn Johns, Michael Lindsay-Hogg
- Looking out over London
- **The realization:** We could do it right here
- **Glyn Johns:** "If they wanted to play to a large crowd, why not play on the roof to the whole of the West End"
- Paul's face - hope returning
- Back downstairs
- **Agreement:** Let's do it. Rooftop. Wednesday or Thursday.
- For the first time in weeks, they're excited about something together

## **Overall Arc Across These Scenes**

1. **Dissolution** (19A-19D): Shows Operation Minuet's success - natural tensions amplified to breaking point
2. **Breaking Point** (19C): George quits - the consequence
3. **Self-Awareness** (19D): They understand what's happening but struggle to fix it
4. **Revival** (19E-19F): Billy Preston and Apple Studios give them hope
5. **Solution** (19G): The rooftop concert as their way forward

## **Why This Works Better Than Single Scene**

- **More dramatically accurate**: The Get Back sessions weren't just grim - they had real highs
- **Uses actual quotes**: Grounds the screenplay in reality
- **Shows complexity**: The Beatles weren't just dissolving - they were fighting to stay together
- **Serves multiple arcs**: Paul's control issues, George's frustration, John's detachment, Billy as savior
- **Supports premise**: Operation Minuet amplified existing problems, but didn't completely destroy them
- **Better movie experience**: Varied emotional beats, humor, tension, hope, despair, and ultimately redemption attempt
