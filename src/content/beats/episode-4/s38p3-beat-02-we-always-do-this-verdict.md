The same situation, worse. More bitter. George barely present. Ringo: 'We always do this.' Same tone. Same words. 'But the meaning has completely changed. This is what they do now. This is who they've become.' Then again: 'We always do this.' He looks around. Paul defeated. John angry. George already gone. Ringo picks up his drumsticks. Doesn't say anything else. Just waits. 'The canary has stopped singing.'

The same words now mean the opposite. Not 'we'll find our way' but 'this is what we are.' The repetition itself — third time is silence — is a beat that cannot be improved upon.

The verdict has been delivered. Not by John's rage or Paul's frustration but by Ringo's quiet recognition. The canary has stopped singing.

Ringo's function as peacekeeper is an extension of his immunity — he can defuse tension because he doesn't carry it. His statement is simple truth, not diplomacy. The immunity expressed as clarity. Ringo sees permanent condition because he doesn't share it. His third repetition is silence — most complete expression of immunity in series. He can see it because it isn't happening to him.

George 'looks like he's already left' after verdict. The exit is forming.

Direct mirror of b01 — same words, opposite meaning. The episode's finest formal device and the series' best use of repetition as structural argument.
