John and Paul arguing about the 'Get Back' middle eight. Going in circles. George steps forward without speaking — plays a different chord progression. 'Subtle but transformative.' The song clicks. Paul: 'Yes! That's it! See, I told you we just needed...' Already turning to John, taking credit. John: 'That's better. Much better.' Neither looks at George. Neither acknowledges who found it. George doesn't protest. 'A small, knowing smile plays on his lips. He clocks it. He understands now.'

The middle eight has no solution. John and Paul are trapped in their argument. George solves it. Without words, without argument, without claiming the solution afterward. The solution is immediately absorbed into the dynamic as if it came from elsewhere.

George has understood something final. 'That's the moment. Not anger, not frustration. Just quiet recognition. He's proven his point, and no one saw it. That silence becomes his decision.'

Creative invisibility as a structural condition — contribution absorbed without acknowledgment. George possesses the solution. The solution immediately belongs to the dynamic that excluded him from producing it. The invisibility is not malice. That's worse.

Direct mirror of b01 — same words, opposite meaning. The episode's finest formal device and the series' best use of repetition as structural argument.
