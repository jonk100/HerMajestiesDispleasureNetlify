Paul trying to bridge the gap: 'No, no, come on.' You always get annoyed when I say that. I'm trying to help, you know. But I always hear myself annoying you, and I'm trying...' George: 'No, you're not annoying me. You don't annoy me anymore.' The 'anymore' hanging in the air. 'As if George has moved past annoyance into something else — apathy, perhaps.'

George moves past conflict. 'You don't annoy me anymore.' Not resolution, not reconciliation — departure from emotional register altogether. Apathy is the wrong word. Completion is closer. George has finished being affected.

George capitulation as the most complete form of withdrawal. 'Okay, well, I don't mind. I'll play whatever you want me to play. Or I won't play at all if you don't want me to play. Now, whatever it is that will please you, I'll do it. But I don't think you really know what that one is.' Paul's face showing he feels blow. The impasse is complete. Instruments eventually put down. Paul: 'Let's do another song.'

The word 'anymore' has changed the relationship's grammar. Paul can no longer affect George enough to produce irritation. The exit is now visible to both of them.

Direct mirror of b01 — same words, opposite meaning. The episode's finest formal device and the series' best use of repetition as structural argument.
