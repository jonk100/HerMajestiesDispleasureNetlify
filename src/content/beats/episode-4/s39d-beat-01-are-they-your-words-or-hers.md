The harmony on 'Get Back' completely broken. John and Paul arguing about the middle eight. Going in circles. George steps forward without speaking — plays a different chord progression. 'Subtle but transformative.' The song clicks. Paul: 'Yes! That's it! See, I told you we just needed...' Already turning to John, taking credit. John: 'That's better. Much better.' Neither looks at George. Neither acknowledges who found it. George doesn't protest. 'A small, knowing smile plays on his lips. He clocks it. He understands now.'

The same words now mean the opposite. Not 'we'll find our way' but 'this is what we are.' The repetition itself — third time is silence — is a beat that cannot be improved upon.

The verdict has been delivered. Not by John's rage or Paul's frustration but by Ringo's quiet recognition. The canary has stopped singing.

Paul taking credit and John accepting it — both genuinely engaged with the music, both genuinely not seeing George. The invisibility is not malice. That's worse.

George's decision — quiet recognition becoming departure. 'That silence becomes his decision.' The exit is decided here, in a single quiet 'Right' after solving a problem no one credited him for. This is the scene that makes sc41 legible — not rage, not frustration, just quiet recognition. George has understood something final. 'That's the moment. Not anger, not frustration. Just quiet recognition. He's proven his point, and no one saw it. That silence becomes his decision.'

Direct mirror of b01 — same words, opposite meaning. The episode's finest formal device and the series' best use of repetition as structural argument.
