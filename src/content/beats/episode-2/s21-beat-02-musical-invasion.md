Music fades in. It's The Beatles. "Norwegian Wood" - George's sitar drifts over John's acoustic guitar, the lyrics oblique and sophisticated.

Philip freezes. He listens not with curiosity but with assessment. The military man encountering something he cannot categorize or control.

He crosses the room and turns the volume up a fraction. The music fills the space, clashing with the room's perfect order. The sitar's Eastern tones, the abstract lyrics, the sophisticated chord changes - all violate the room's institutional discipline.

A lyric drifts by, oblique and abstract, earnest in a way Philip finds immediately suspect. The orderly space has been invaded by artistic complexity that resists military categorization.
