John bristles, strumming harder. "You know what I'm sick of? Being told there are certain thoughts you're only allowed to have quietly."

The working-class bite returns. John's intellectualism isn't just philosophy; it's class rebellion. The idea that some thoughts must remain private is exactly the kind of institutional control he's always rejected.

Paul escalates to the heart of their creative partnership: "I spend all day stitching this thing together. You spend five minutes unpicking it because you like the sound it makes."

The accusation lands. John's intellectual playfulness isn't just honest; it's destructive to the collective enterprise Paul builds. But John's class identity gives him moral authority - he's speaking for everyone who's been told to stay in their place.
