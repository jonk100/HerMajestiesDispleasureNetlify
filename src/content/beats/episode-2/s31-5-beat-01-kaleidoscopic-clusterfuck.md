Buckingham Palace conference room. Dark wood paneling, institutional silence. Massive oak table dominates. Portrait of Queen Victoria looks distinctly unimpressed.

Sir Derek Finch-Amory paces back and forth, carries wooden pointer like weapon of war. Nigel and Clive sit opposite. Nigel looks like preparing own execution. Clive looks like about to be given field promotion.

On easel stands Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band album cover. Looks absurdly vibrant in sepia-toned room.

"Right. Let's talk about this kaleidoscopic clusterfuck, shall we?"

He gestures wildly at album cover with pointer. "Nigel! Clive! Explain yourselves! Why is there a Shirley Temple doll wearing a sweater that says 'Welcome the Rolling Stones'? Is that a threat? Is it an invitation to a coup?"

The cultural revolution has arrived. Traditional institution meets psychedelic art.
