Nigel deliberately hits discordant note. PING.

"Not like that other racket."

Paul pauses. "What other racket?"

"Oh, you know. The radio. Some of the new stuff coming out." Nigel leans in, like trying not to gossip. "And all this talk about your mate and... Jesus." A shrug. "My wife says it's in the papers now."

Paul goes still. "What's in the papers?"

"Ah, nothing. Just a quote. They always pick a quote." Nigel waves dismissively. "And that last record… what was it? The one with the sitar?"

"Norwegian Wood."

The psychological operation begins. Nigel creates artificial division between Paul's "nice tunes" \" and John's "other racket," while introducing external controversy that threatens their success.
