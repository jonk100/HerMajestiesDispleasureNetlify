The newspaper office - chaos of typewriters, phones, smoke. A junior reporter stands at editor's desk with Maureen Cleave's thoughtful article.

"It's thoughtful. Bit long."

"Thoughtful doesn't sell."

The fundamental conflict established immediately. This isn't about malice; it's about business. The editor scans the page looking not for meaning but for marketability.

"What's the hook?"

The reporter tries to explain substance - religion, fame, meaning. The editor wants something simpler, something that sells. The stage is set for nuance to become casualty of commerce.
