"We're conducting a psychological ricochet operation against a major cultural target! We can't do it with a museum piece!" Clive's desperate appeal.

Percy's response is devastating in its institutional simplicity: "Museum pieces have inventories."

Then the final nail: "Your 'cultural target' has fans." He pauses for effect. "Fans steal."

The cultural warfare theorist completely defeated by inventory control logic. The Beatles aren't a national security threat; they're a theft risk. The mission isn't about protecting Britain; it's about protecting government property.

"You can do it with what you're given, Barrow. Just like everyone else. Sign here. And here. Initial there."

Nigel walks away, having accepted their fate. "Don't forget the original packaging, Clive."

Percy returns to polishing his device, guardian of mediocrity, utterly content in his perfectly ordered, perfectly useless world.
