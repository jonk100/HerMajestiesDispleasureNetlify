Clive deploys the Klaus persona for the first time against actual humans. Black turtleneck (still itchy), beret at a 'thoughtful angle.' He attempts the continental loiter outside a Chelsea gallery.

Nigel watches from the surveillance van with a cold cheese sandwich. 'Oh, heavens. He's doing the loiter.' The gap between Clive's self-assessment and reality is now physically visible. The Klaus persona, a theory of infiltration that has never encountered actual humans, is being tested in the field.

Nigel's sandwich commentary provides the scene's grounding: 'At least the sandwich has structural integrity.' As the bread falls apart, so does Clive's performance.
