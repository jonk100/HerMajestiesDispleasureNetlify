'Klaus von Klaus. Critic. Visionary.' Clive deploys his persona against John at the Indica Gallery. 'The cute one, the quiet one, the drummer — they drag you down into the ocean of mediocrity.'

John's mouth twitches — a laugh he refuses to give. 'Bit harsh on Ringo.' The irony is that Clive is accidentally saying things John is already thinking, using the worst possible vocabulary to say them. The black canvas seems as if it might finally confess what it is.

John's response is neither impressed nor dismissive — he's amused and faintly interested, which is the worst possible outcome for Clive. It means John is processing Klaus as entertainment rather than authority. The operation is in John's presence, but John is handling it on his own terms.
