Brian watches them, complicated expression on his face. It's pride, mixed with profound sense of obsolescence.

He came here to talk about logistics, contracts, television appearances - the world outside this room. But he can see with painful clarity that their world is now inside this room.

And outside this room, the world is beginning to sharpen its knives.

The role reversal is complete. The man who built their career, who negotiated their contracts, who managed their breakthrough - now stands outside glass watching them create something he cannot comprehend, cannot participate in, cannot control.

The architect has become caretaker. The manager has become observer. The essential man has become peripheral.
