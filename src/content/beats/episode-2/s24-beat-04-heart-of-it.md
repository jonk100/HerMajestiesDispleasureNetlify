"Shake them up? Sir, the world's scary enough. If I may be bold… you're the one holding it together. The heart of it."

Paul stiffens. The validation becomes burden. Nigel isn't just praising Paul's melodic gifts; he's assigning Paul responsibility for the band's emotional connection to audience.

The psychological operation deepens. Paul isn't just one of four equal creators; he's the essential heart holding together something that could otherwise become frightening. John's experimental direction isn't just different; it's threatening to scare away the audience Paul serves.

"If you let the other one turn it all into noise… well… they'll stop loving you, won't they?"

The words land cleanly. Surgically. Paul's identity as emotional anchor becomes weapon against artistic evolution.
