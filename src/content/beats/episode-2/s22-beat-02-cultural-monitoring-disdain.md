Nigel slides the perfectly completed form across the counter. Percy picks it up, inspects it with suspicion, then grunts.

"'Cultural monitoring.'" The look of disgust is profound. "We used to tap actual embassies."

Percy's contempt establishes the institutional hierarchy. Cultural monitoring is beneath the dignity of real intelligence work. The veteran gatekeeper has seen better missions, more important targets.

"'Miniature.'" He scoffs. "This isn't a bloody Bond film, Barrow. This is His Majesty's government. We have standards. And budgets."

Clive's high-tech aspirations crash into Percy's institutional pride. The cultural warfare theorist meets the man who believes real intelligence involves embassies and proper procedure.
