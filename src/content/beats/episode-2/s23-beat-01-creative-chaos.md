The legendary studio transformed into mad scientist's laboratory. John stands over dozen tape recorders, each playing strange loops - distorted laugh, seagull's cry, backwards sitar.

"Right, on my signal, you push that fader up. Paul, you ready on the wine glass?"

Paul holds pencil poised over glass of red wine, ready to create specific ringing tone. George sits cross-legged with sitar, finally at home. Ringo taps steady, hypnotic beat.

"Sounds like a thousand seagulls being tortured," Paul laughs.

"Exactly! It's the sound of the void, Macca! The sound of what happens after you die! Or before you're born. Haven't decided yet."

The four-headed creative organism operates at peak unity. They're not just making music; they're inventing new language of sound.
