Philip reads the fake letters with clinical pleasure: 'as a concerned Christian and lifelong reader...' / 'if Mr. Lennon has no respect for the Church, he has no respect for the Crown.' He adds a new layer of newspaper to his Minuet folder. 'A new proof.'

The Private Secretary asks if a formal response is required. Philip taps the letters column: 'This is the response. The country is speaking.'

None of the voices are real. The double irony is complete — Philip ordered the operation that created the evidence he now cites as authentic public sentiment. He has been confirmed in his position by fabricated evidence of his own indirect manufacture.

The operation is self-reinforcing. This is the mechanism Clive described at the bus stop, now operating at the highest level of institutional power.
