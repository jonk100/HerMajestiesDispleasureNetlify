Stung by Nigel's institutional authority, Clive escalates to grander theory. "A McLuhan thing."

Nigel corrects him: "I believe it's 'message.'"

Clive seizes the correction: "Yes. Precisely. That."

Then the formulation emerges: "The medium is the messiah." Media theory becomes religious metaphor. The Beatles aren't just popular; they're prophets of a new belief system where the delivery mechanism matters more than the content.

Nigel watches this intellectual escalation with weary patience.
