Paul raises the Jesus remark with diplomatic anxiety, but John responds with genuine indifference: 'It's just Maureen. She's not a vicar.' His casual dismissal isn't stupidity — it's a principled rejection of the idea that thoughts should have a public/private register.

'People are only upset by facts if they were using them as furniture,' John explains, delivering the line with working-class directness. He genuinely doesn't see the mechanism Paul fears, operating as if words can remain innocent even when spoken publicly. The creative partnership's central tension is now fully engaged: John making the fire, Paul managing the burns.
