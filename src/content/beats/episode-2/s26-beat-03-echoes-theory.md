"The funny bit is—" John considers. "I do say things. People just… take the wrong bit."

"Yes! You give zee world a match and zey light zee wrong room on fire." Klaus gleeful.

Clive taps air with two fingers, conducting invisible choir. "You do not fight zee newspapers. Zey are too stupid. Zey eat your words and zey make new words. So. You do not give zem explanations. You give zem… echoes."

"Echoes."

"Zee perfect outrage is not shouted by you. It is shouted by… respectable mouths. Husbands. Mothers. Priests." Small, pleased nod. "Ordinary people. Zey write in. Zey beg for punishment."

John studies him. This is absurd. And yet—

The communication problem John has been experiencing is reframed as strategic weapon. Misinterpretation becomes deliberate influence mechanism. The echo chamber becomes tool of cultural warfare.
