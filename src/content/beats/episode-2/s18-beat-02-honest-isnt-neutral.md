Paul counters John's indifference with the episode's thesis line: 'Honest isn't neutral. You say a thing and it stops being yours.' He articulates the mechanism of celebrity with precision — the dispossession that comes with public speech.

This isn't conservatism; it's a theory of how words operate once they enter the world. The principle applies to John's remark, to Nigel's surveillance reports, to Clive's fake letters, to the press editor's pull quote. Everything in the episode is about what happens when words stop belonging to the person who said them.

John can no longer claim innocence through indifference — he's been told exactly what happens, even if he doesn't believe it yet.
