John's working-class defense meets Paul's institutional concern. "Is that not allowed then?"

"It's not about allowed! It's about what happens when they start taking you seriously."

John studies Paul's face, genuinely confused: "What's that supposed to mean?"

Paul delivers the devastating truth: "It means they're not looking at four lads from Liverpool anymore. They're looking at you. And when they look at you... they're not looking at us."

John's voice cracks slightly: "I was just talking..."

"You were being honest. And honesty changes things. It changes how people see you. It changes how they see us."

Beat. Both realize the same terrifying truth.

"We could actually lose this, couldn't we?" John whispers.

"Lose what?"

"All of it."
