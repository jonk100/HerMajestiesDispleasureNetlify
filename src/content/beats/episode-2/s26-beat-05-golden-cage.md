"Destroying the song…"

"Leave zee structure. Embrace zee chaos. Zeh Beatles are a cage, John Lennon. A golden cage. But Klaus sees zeh bird who wishes to fly."

John nods slowly. "You're a bit weird, Klaus."

"Bowing slightly. Thank you."

John starts to walk, then turns back. "Where you from, anyway?"

"Germany."

"Which part."

"Zee... main part."

"But you might be right. About the chaos bit."

John walks away, thoughtful. The black painting reflects faintly in his glasses.

The final psychological blow delivered. The Beatles aren't just collaborators; they're cage limiting John's artistic evolution. Success isn't achievement; it's imprisonment. Chaos isn't destruction; it's freedom.

Clive watches him go, pulls small notebook from pocket and writes neatly: "SUBJECT RECEPTIVE TO NIHILISM. ACCENT FLAWLESS."
