Philip frowns. "What is this supposed to be?"

He listens longer. A chord change surprises him. He doesn't like that it does. The music refuses to follow predictable patterns, to resolve cleanly, to behave as disciplined sound should.

The song builds - voices overlapping, refusing to resolve cleanly. Each artistic choice becomes a violation of military expectation in Philip's mind. The complexity isn't sophistication; it's insubordination.

Philip exhales sharply through his nose. The military man cannot comprehend art as expression, only as discipline failure. The overlapping voices, the unresolved chords, the abstract lyrics - all evidence of poor training, lack of control.
