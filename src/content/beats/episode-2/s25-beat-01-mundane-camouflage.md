Perfectly ordinary London street. At small café table sit Nigel and Clive in painfully unremarkable civilian clothes. Tea cups steam between them.

Under newspaper: second clipping - foreign, American. "MORE POPULAR THAN JESUS" visible before Nigel's hand covers them.

"All right. This is it. This is where it becomes theatre," Clive vibrates.

"We are drinking tea," Nigel does not look up.

"Exactly. Performance art. The mundane as camouflage."

"Please don't say 'performance art' in public."

The surveillance operation immediately becomes absurd theater. Clive's theoretical enthusiasm transforms institutional procedure into performance. The mundane becomes camouflage for something that doesn't need camouflage.
