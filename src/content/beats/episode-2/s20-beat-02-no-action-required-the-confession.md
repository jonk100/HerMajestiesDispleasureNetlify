Alone in the surveillance van, Nigel removes the headphones and writes in his notebook: 'NO ACTION REQUIRED.' The same words from the blackboard in sc17, but here without the comic frame, without Clive watching.

They don't feel like a professional verdict. They feel like a confession.

'It doesn't feel like a conclusion. It feels like a confession.' The scene earns that line because the blackboard scene built it. The institutional language is the same, but the emotional content has completely changed. Nigel adds 'YET' again — this time not as Clive's paranoid amendment but as his own reluctant acknowledgment.

He quickly scribbles out a doodled sad man in a suit, a self-portrait that might be discoverable evidence. The operation has power over John, but John's private moment has power over Nigel. The surveillance is being surveilled by its own conscience.
