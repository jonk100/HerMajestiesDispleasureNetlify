"You're telling me I should get vicars to do my arguing."

"I am telling you zee vicar is already arguing. He just needs a sentence."

John looks back at black canvas. The idea is already forming. Not a plan—an appetite.

The dangerous recognition lands. John understands that he doesn't need to fight directly, that he can plant ideas and let others wage the battles for him. The vicars, mothers, husbands - they become his unwitting soldiers.

The manipulation theory becomes practical strategy. John's frustration with being misunderstood transforms into temptation to deliberately misunderstand himself, knowing others will amplify the confusion.

The black canvas seems to offer possibility rather than emptiness now.
