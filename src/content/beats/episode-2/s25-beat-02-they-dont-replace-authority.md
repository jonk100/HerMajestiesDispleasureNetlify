'They don't replace authority. They make it irrelevant,' Clive declares after the surveillance device battery dies. The absence of anything to surveil becomes the subject of the episode's most substantive intellectual exchange.

Nigel counters: 'They become it. Accidentally.' The series' most direct intellectual exchange about what the Beatles actually represent. Clive sees dissolution. Nigel sees accidental coronation. Both are correct. Neither can act on their correctness.

'It matters what other people decide they meant,' Nigel offers, his most precise articulation of the operation's actual mechanism. 'Let's never do this again.' He walks away.

Clive sits alone with the American clipping visible. 'If there's no voice, you manufacture one.' The seed of the letter-writing operation is planted. The operation escalates without institutional authorization.
