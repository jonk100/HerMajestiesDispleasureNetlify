Clive removes device from briefcase. Far too large to be discreet. He places it on café table. It does not belong there.

Waitress pauses mid-step, staring. "...is that a radio?"

"Experimental."

Nigel instinctively reaches for identification, then stops himself. "It's German."

Waitress walks away, unsettled.

Clive fits oversized headphones over ears. "I'm in."

Nigel leans closer despite himself. "And?"

Static crackles. Distant, unintelligible voice flickers.

"Wait… there. Did you hear that?"

"Hear what?"

"Intent."

The covert operation has become public spectacle. The German "experimental" device attracts attention rather than avoiding it. The surveillance equipment itself becomes more conspicuous than any target.
