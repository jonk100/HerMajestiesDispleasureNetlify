Clive cites institutional authority with triumph: 'Culture is security now. Didn't you read the memorandum?' The bureaucratic weapon deployed, the argument seemingly won.

Nigel's response stops the room: 'I wrote it.' The pause that follows is not comedic but resigned. He's not proud of the irony; he's stating a fact about himself. The reluctant operative is revealed as complicit — he helped build the very institutional machinery he now resists.
