John recovers with bravado: "You worry too much. People can tell the difference between a bloke thinking out loud and a sermon. They're not idiots."

Paul's response reveals his true role: "I hope so. Because I'm the one who's got to walk it back when they decide you've declared war on God."

The dynamic shifts. Paul isn't just creative partner; he's damage control. John gets to be honest; Paul gets to handle the consequences. The intellectual freedom John enjoys has a practical cost that Paul pays.

"I'm not declaring war on anything," John insists. "I'm saying what everyone's thinking."

"Everyone isn't thinking it into a microphone," Paul counters.
