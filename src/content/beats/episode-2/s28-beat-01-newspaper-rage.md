Long table laid with silver and porcelain. Everything gleams. Everything is correct. Queen and Prince Philip sit opposite in perfect silence.

Philip reads newspaper. Headline screams: "BEATLEMANIA HITS JAPAN." Beside it sits smaller clipping, foreign, folded until soft at creases. Just visible: "MORE POPULAR THAN JESUS." He is seething.

Queen absorbed in crossword puzzle.

"They're treated like heads of state. It's embarrassing."

Queen without looking up: "Four down. 'A chaotic disturbance.' Six letters."

The royal contrast established. Philip's cultural rage becomes private obsession, Queen's crossword becomes symbol of institutional indifference to his concerns.
