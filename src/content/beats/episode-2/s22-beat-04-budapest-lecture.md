"Battery life is approximately ninety minutes, provided you don't use it in the damp." Percy begins his lecture. "The last agent who used this in the damp, a chap in Budapest, caused a short. Do you know what a replacement valve costs for a '53 Telefunken?"

Nigel sighs, pure existential despair. "Go on then, Percy."

"Fourteen pounds and sixpence! A month's wages for some people. And he didn't return it in the original packaging. Had to fill out a Form 21-A for non-compliance. Truly dreadful business."

The institutional mindset revealed in full. Not the mission, not the agent, not the target - the valve, the packaging, the form. The bureaucracy's priorities are crystal clear: equipment preservation over operational success.

Percy hands Clive tangle of wires and enormous headphones, continuing his lecture about transmitter screws and travel stipends.
