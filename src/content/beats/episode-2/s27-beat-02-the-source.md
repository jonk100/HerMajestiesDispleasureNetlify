Nigel walks to small record player behind counter, gently drops needle on spinning LP. Complex, mesmerizing sitar melody fills quiet shop. Sound impossibly rich and fluid.

George looks up, captivated. Approaches counter. "What is that?"

"That is Ravi Shankar. The source."

George listens, rapt. Gulf between this and his own playing feels immense. "It's… a whole other world. I've been… dabbling with one. A sitar. But it doesn't sound like that."

Nigel looks up, expression of gentle, academic interest. The authentic music establishes Nigel's authority while revealing George's limitation. The "source" becomes both musical origin and spiritual destination.
