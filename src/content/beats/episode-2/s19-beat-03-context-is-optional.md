The reporter makes one last stand for journalistic ethics: "Out of context."

The editor's response is casual, devastating: "Context is optional."

The shrug says everything. This isn't a moral failure; it's standard practice. The distortion isn't malicious - it's procedural. The business of news requires simplification, and simplification requires sacrifice.

The editor scribbles the headline: "LENNON QUESTIONS CHRISTIANITY."

"Should we at least run the rest with it?" the reporter asks, hoping for balance.

"Of course. We're not animals." The concession is minimal, almost insulting. The appearance of integrity maintained while the reality of distortion is accepted.
