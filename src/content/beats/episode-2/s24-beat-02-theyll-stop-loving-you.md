'You're the one holding it together. The heart of it,' Nigel tells Paul. 'If you let the other one turn it all into noise... they'll stop loving you.'

The words land surgically. Nigel packs his tools and exits. Paul in the sudden silence. The lonely chord hangs in the air. A lyric is crossed out — something more adventurous replaced with 'I need my love to be here.'

The manipulation has succeeded so completely it has become invisible. It is now inside the music. Paul has made a creative choice that the operation intended. He believes it is his own. The operation has achieved its first genuine result — and it was Nigel, the reluctant operative, who delivered it.
