"That's the badger. Gave me a headache, tell you the truth. No offense, sir. Just… a bloke gets home from work, he doesn't want to be educated, does he? He wants to be cheered up."

Paul absorbs this. His smile fades. This is his deepest fear, unspoken until now.

Nigel's working-class authenticity becomes weapon. The piano tuner speaks for the ordinary people Paul fears losing. The critique isn't about artistic merit; it's about emotional service. Music's purpose is to cheer up tired workers, not challenge them intellectually.

"John—my mate—he thinks we need to challenge people. Shake them up a bit."

Nigel shakes head sadly, like mechanic diagnosing terminal fault. The division is complete: John wants to shake people up; Paul cheers them up.
