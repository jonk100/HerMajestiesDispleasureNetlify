Prince Philip pours himself a drink with naval precision in his perfectly orderly private sitting room. Everything is placed, not lived in. The space embodies institutional control.

The BBC announcer's voice intrudes: "—and in other news, Mr. John Lennon of The Beatles has offered further remarks this week on religion and modern life, prompting the usual columnists to clear their throats."

The orderly world is breached. Lennon's name, the subject of institutional concern, enters Philip's sanctuary through the wireless radio.

The stage is set for the collision between Philip's military precision and the cultural disruption he cannot yet comprehend.
