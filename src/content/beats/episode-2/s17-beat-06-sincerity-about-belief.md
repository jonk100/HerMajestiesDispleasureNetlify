Nigel diagnoses Clive's ambition: "You want this to matter."

Clive's response cracks the theoretical armor. For a moment, he's utterly sincere: "Someone should be paying attention. Not because he's evil. Because he's believed."

Then the recovery, faster: "And belief is the only thing we don't have a file cabinet for."

The vulnerability lands. Nigel softens slightly. "We are." But he maintains institutional boundaries: "But we're not going to invent a crisis because we're bored."

Clive's motivation reveals itself as more complex than pure ambition - genuine concern filtered through institutional ambition.
