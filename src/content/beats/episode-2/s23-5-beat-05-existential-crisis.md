Later, Clive stands by himself, still in "Klaus" persona, attempting to look contemplative while sketching. Two teenage girls walk past, whispering and pointing.

"Who's that?"

"I think he's one of Paul's art friends. He looks... intense."

Clive overhears, straightens up, puffing chest out slightly. He believes his cover is working perfectly.

From surveillance van, Nigel watches through binoculars. He lowers them slowly.

"We're spying on art critics. I'm a grown man eating a sandwich in a van while my partner pretends to be German to influence album cover design through bad drawings."

He looks at his reflection in rearview mirror.

"What happened to my life?"

The institutional man has reached the point of self-awareness. The professional detachment has cracked, revealing existential despair. The bread falls apart in his hands. Of course it does.
