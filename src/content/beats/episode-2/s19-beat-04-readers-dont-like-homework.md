The editor lights a cigarette, the ritual of decision complete. "But that line goes on page one."

The reporter nods, uneasy. As he leaves, the editor calls after him with the final justification: "And trim the philosophy. Readers don't like homework."

The transformation is complete. Lennon's thoughtful exploration of fame, belief, and cultural position will be reduced to a single inflammatory phrase on page one. The complexity that gave the statement its meaning will be removed because readers don't want to work.

The institutional decision has been made. The distortion isn't just accepted; it's mandated as good business practice. The public will receive exactly what the market demands - simple, provocative, context-free.
