The argument loses its intellectual armor when Paul says, 'When they look at you, they're not looking at us.' John's confident indifference finally cracks.

'We could actually lose this, couldn't we?' John looks around the rehearsal space — 'All of it.' For the first moment, his indifference becomes fear. This isn't John and Paul against each other anymore; it's John and Paul against the future.

The unresolved chord hangs in the air. Neither of them fills the silence. The future has entered the room and both of them have let it in. On the radio, a news item about 'a remark by John Lennon' begins to play, but neither stops to hear it. The world is already outside the room.
