Philip stands there for a beat, drink untouched. He looks at the radio as if it has failed a basic test.

"No discipline."

The final verdict. The Beatles' artistic sophistication isn't just different; it's deficient. The complex chord changes, the abstract lyrics, the innovative instrumentation - all evidence of poor training, lack of self-control, fundamental character failure.

He straightens his cuff, already done. The verdict has been delivered. Case closed.

The military man has successfully reframed artistic evolution as moral deficiency. The Beatles aren't pushing boundaries; they're lacking discipline. The institutional framework remains intact, having absorbed the cultural threat by redefining it as personal failure.
