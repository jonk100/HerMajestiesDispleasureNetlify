Philip has delivered his verdict on the Beatles and their cultural impact, expecting the Queen to agree with his institutional anxiety about 'no discipline.'

The Queen responds simply: 'I rather liked Yesterday.' Philip stares at her, betrayed. He snaps the newspaper back up. The episode's best single-line joke and the series' thesis in miniature: the Queen has already answered the question the operation is trying to answer, and answered it correctly, and Philip cannot hear it.

The most authoritative response is the casual one. Philip is alone with his verdict. The Queen has demonstrated the correct relationship to the Beatles — aesthetic appreciation, no institutional anxiety — and Philip cannot access it.
