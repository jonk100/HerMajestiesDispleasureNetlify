Beat. John's smile flickers for the first time. The smallest flash of insecurity, quickly turned into a joke.

"I'm not Jesus, Paul."

The joke lands too hard. John's confidence has cracked. Paul's analysis of public perception has found its target. John understands, suddenly, that he's not in control of how his words will be received.

"That's not the bit people will hear," Paul responds.

John laughs but it's forced. The intellectual philosopher has become the potential prophet, and he doesn't like it. The working-class lad from Liverpool is being transformed into something he never intended to be.
