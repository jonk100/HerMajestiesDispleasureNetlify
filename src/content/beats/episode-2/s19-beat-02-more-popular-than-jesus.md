The junior reporter underlines a passage about religion and meaning. The editor dismisses it: "No, the other bit."

He flips the page. Finds the line.

"More popular than Jesus."

The reporter hesitates, tries to explain context: "That's not really the point—"

"It is now."

The moment of transformation. Lennon's thoughtful exploration of fame and belief becomes a single inflammatory phrase. The editor grabs a pencil, circles the line with finality.

"That's your pull quote."

The business decision has been made. Truth will be secondary to circulation.
