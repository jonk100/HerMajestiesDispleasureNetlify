Clive opens briefcase enough to peer inside. Tangled mess of wires, valves, dials.

"I've recalibrated the receiver. Narrowed the frequency band. Reduced interference."

"From what?"

"Reality."

Nigel checks watch. Across street: terraced house, curtains drawn, no movement.

"We are listening for...?"

"Influence. Intent. The psychic residue of celebrity."

Nigel closes eyes briefly. "We are listening for sound, Clive."

"Yes, but sound adjacent."

The surveillance operation has transcended physical monitoring. Clive's cultural warfare theory now includes metaphysical detection. They're not just listening to conversations; they're attempting to capture the invisible traces of influence.
