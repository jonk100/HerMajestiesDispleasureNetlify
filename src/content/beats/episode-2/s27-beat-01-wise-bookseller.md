Cramped Soho bookshop, floor-to-ceiling shelves with esoteric texts. Nigel stands behind counter, transformed. Gone is weary bureaucrat. He wears comfortable cardigan, half-moon reading glasses, cultivated bohemian air. Picture of man who has found peace among books.

George enters, fresh from Rubber Soul success but searching uncertainty hangs about him. He used sitar on "Norwegian Wood" but knows he only scratched surface - texture, not art form.

George wanders Eastern Philosophy section, fingers trailing along spines. Practical Lessons in Yoga, Science of Pranayama, Book of Tea - all considered and returned. Finally Alan Watts' The Way of Zen, slows on paragraph about silence, replaces with sigh.

Nigel observes from behind glasses, timing approach. The spiritual seeker and the wise mentor positioned for perfect manipulation.
