Clive spots target: group of art critics discussing sculpture. He approaches with what he believes is cool, artistic stride.

"Ahem. Excuse me. I am... Klaus." The accent is terrible, inconsistent.

The critics turn. One raises eyebrow: "Klaus? Klaus who?"

"Klaus... Schmidt. Art critic. I have been published in... many journals." He gestures vaguely, nearly knocking over wine glass.

"I'm in the art world. I don't believe I've heard of a Klaus Schmidt."

"Ah! That is because I write for... German publications. Very influential. In Berlin. And Munich. And... other German places."

The cultural infiltration fails immediately. Clive's German persona is transparent, his claims vague, his presence awkward. The sophisticated art world sees exactly what he is: incompetent spy trying too hard.
