Philip listens to Norwegian Wood after a BBC announcer references John's further remarks. 'What is this supposed to be?' A chord change surprises him. He doesn't like that it does.

'It's just noise. No discipline.' The radio snapped off mid-phrase. The verdict is delivered by a man who was briefly, involuntarily moved and cannot allow it. The snap of the radio is the scene's best action — a violent suppression of an involuntary response.

Philip's wound is confirmed as aesthetic as well as institutional. The music did something to him that he won't acknowledge. His 'no discipline' is a self-directed verdict as much as a musical one.
