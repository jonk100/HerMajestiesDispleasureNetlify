Clive pulls out another sketch. Mock album cover with each Beatles head replaced by different sad-looking Victorian monument.

"Think about it. The burden of fame. The weight of history. The fountain as metaphor for..."

"That's... interesting. But if you'll excuse us, we were just discussing the Francis Bacon retrospective."

They start backing away slowly. Clive, convinced he's making progress, follows them.

"But the cultural insurgency! The semiotic overload! Have you considered that the Shirley Temple doll on the Sgt. Pepper's cover might actually be..."

The critics practically run into another room. Clive stands there, sketchbook in hand, convinced of his intellectual superiority.

"They are intimidated by my insights. Typical bourgeois reaction to revolutionary thought."

The operation has failed completely, but Clive's delusion prevents him from seeing it. The institutional man has become performance artist without knowing it.
