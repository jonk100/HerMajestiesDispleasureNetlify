"They'll stop loving you, won't they?"

The words land cleanly. Surgically.

Nigel returns to tuning. PING. Perfect pitch. He packs his tools.

"Stick to the love songs, Mr. McCartney. That's what keeps the lights on."

Nigel exits.

Paul remains seated in sudden silence. He looks down at his guitar. He plays single chord. It sounds lonely.

He picks up pen and scribbles aggressively on lyric sheet, crossing out stranger, more abstract line. He writes instead:

"I need my love to be here."

The psychological operation complete. Paul's artistic evolution has been weaponized against him. The fear of losing love has replaced artistic courage. The melody maker has been trapped by his own gift for emotional connection.
