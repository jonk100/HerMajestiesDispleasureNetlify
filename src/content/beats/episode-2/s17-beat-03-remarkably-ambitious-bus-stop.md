Clive unfolds his bus stop theory with prophetic certainty: a girl reads John's remark, repeats it at the bus stop, a vicar overhears, preaches about it Sunday, and suddenly the social order begins to dissolve. The chain reaction is absurd, specific, and terrifyingly plausible.

Nigel's response is perfect dryness: 'A remarkably ambitious bus stop.' The line is accurate, observational, and completely useless because Clive isn't making a logical argument — he's making a prophetic one. And he's right.
