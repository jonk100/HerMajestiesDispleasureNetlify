Paul's voice gets quieter, more serious. He brings in external authority: "You know what Brian said? 'The Evening Standard isn't a teen magazine, John.' This isn't fan chatter anymore."

John stops strumming. The intellectual debate becomes real. Brian Epstein - the man who manages their entire relationship with the world - is concerned.

"And?" John asks, genuinely uncertain now.

"And Maureen's not writing about our hair. She's writing about your ideas. Your ideas, John."

The stakes have transformed. This isn't about public perception anymore; it's about how institutions view them. They're no longer entertainers; they're thinkers. And thinkers get taken seriously.
