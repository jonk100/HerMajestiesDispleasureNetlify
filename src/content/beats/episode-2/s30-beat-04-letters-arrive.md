CUT TO: Buckingham Palace study. Room built for certainty. Books with matching spines. Polished wood. Quiet ticking clock sounds like verdict.

Philip sits behind desk, newspaper opened like battle map. Not front page. The middle. The part that pretends to be calm.

CLOSE on small column: "LETTERS TO THE EDITOR."

Philip reads. Face still. Pleasure almost clinical.

"As a concerned Christian and lifelong reader, I was appalled to see a pop singer place himself above Our Lord..."

Turns page, finds another. "What example does this set for our youth? If Mr. Lennon has no respect for the Church, he has no respect for the Crown..."

That one lands. Philip's eyes narrow—then soften.

The manufactured voices become authentic evidence. The false outrage becomes institutional proof. The psychological operation achieves its target.
