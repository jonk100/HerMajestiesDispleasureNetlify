"He says Shankar is... particular. He doesn't suffer fools or dilettantes. One must demonstrate a genuine spiritual commitment."

Nigel performs most sophisticated manipulation: using truth. He's not inventing path; merely illuminating it, framing it as challenge worthy of George's deep spirit, while positioning "frantic" Brian Jones as someone who has already started journey.

George looks determined. Not just music anymore; creative and spiritual respect.

"Ravi Shankar. He lives here? In London?"

Nigel takes slip of paper, writes name with fountain pen. "He visits. One has to be… attentive. But I believe a man like Brian Jones would know how to make an introduction, if one were to ask. One seeker to another."

Slides paper across counter. Contains only two words: "Ravi Shankar."

George tucks paper carefully into jacket pocket. Feels like a key.

"The path appears when the student is ready." Nigel bows formally.

George exits, grateful. Nigel turns to operational log disguised as shop ledger. Makes entry: "Vector Identified: B. Jones. Target primed with information regarding subject's tutelage under R. Shankar. Path to spiritual and artistic divergence successfully illuminated. Subject believes discovery to be his own."
