The aide approaches quietly, face pale. 'I'm so sorry, but there's been an accident. It's Mr. Epstein. He's gone.'

John doubles over as if punched. Paul's tears — 'Who's going to hold it together now?'

Ringo: 'He looked after us. Always did.'

George: 'The center's gone. Everything scatters now. Leaves in the wind.'

John: 'We're on our own now.'

The four of them as 'four orphans, suddenly adrift.' The spiritual retreat becoming a prison. This is the episode's emotional center and its most consequential beat.

Brian is dead. The band's center is gone. Four young men who have been protected from the consequences of their own fame are suddenly without their protector. Everything that follows — Apple Corps, the Abbey Road lounge confrontation, the dissolution — flows from this moment. The episode has reached its hinge.
