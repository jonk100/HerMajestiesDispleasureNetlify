The concrete dressing room. Stage clothes. The distant crowd ROAR muted. John staring at the ceiling.

'That's it, then.'

'They weren't there to hear.'

'They were there to scream.'

John: 'I'm done. With this. With pretending we're a band in a circus tent.'

George: 'We can still make records.'

John: 'We can make art.' The word hanging — 'not just songs. Art.'

Paul's long calculation. Then: 'Right. No more tours.'

They decide to stop. Collectively, with Paul's agreement completing the consensus. 'Like we always should have been.' The band is now a studio band. The decision is historical. George: 'Maya.' The illusion named and released. Ringo: 'Still better than a real job.'

They walk out together, not as conquerors, but as survivors choosing a different battlefield.
