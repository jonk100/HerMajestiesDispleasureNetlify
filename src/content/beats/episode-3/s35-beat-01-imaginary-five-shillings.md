The Hammer a Nail piece. Yoko firm — 'you cannot, show does not open until tomorrow.'

John: 'Can I not hammer one nail in? For a price?'

The gallery owner's compromise. John stopping — offering imaginary five shillings and an imaginary nail. 'She watches. The corner of her mouth twitches.' He gets it. He's playing her game. 'Acceptable.'

John miming the hammer, the quiet 'tink, tink, tink.'

Yoko doesn't accommodate him. She sets a condition. He responds to the condition not with money but with play. She recognizes the response. John has met someone who doesn't defer to his fame. This is new. The imaginary nail is the correct first beat of their relationship — a creative negotiation, not a social one.
