Paul staring at 'MUSIC BY PAUL MCCARTNEY' in the cold outside air.

'First time my name's up there without the others.'

George Martin: 'It won't be the last.'

Paul: 'Yeah. Just don't want to lose what we've already got, you know?'

He doesn't know yet that you can't keep both forever. The telegram glimpsed on the desk: 'SUNDAY TIMES REVIEW CONFIRMED. LANGUAGE APPROVED. — F.' No one notices it. We do.

The marquee offers him a separate identity for the first time. He finds it both thrilling and frightening, which is the correct response. The seed of Paul's separateness is planted. 'You can't keep both forever' — the narrator's verdict is the episode's clearest thematic statement about Paul's arc.
