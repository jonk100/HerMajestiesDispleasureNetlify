The signing of Apple Corps. John with a flourish, Paul with care, George with quiet focus, Ringo with relieved smile.

'No more managers telling us what to do.'

Paul: 'Except us telling us what to do.'

George: 'Which is probably worse.'

Ringo: 'Do I get a parking space?'

John trailing off on 'no more Epstein running everything' — a flicker of something. Guilt? Uncertainty? Then the giddiness outside, the real laughter, 'three cats in a bag,' 'album of silence,' 'lunch.'

They incorporate. The structure that is meant to give them freedom will become the field of their dissolution. 'They've just built their own cage.' Apple Corps exists. Clive has a 'centralized target.' The band believes they've conquered the world. The institutional irony is complete.
