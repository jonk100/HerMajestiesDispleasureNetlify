Philip watches American record-burning on a black-and-white television with the focused attention of a general reading battlefield reports. The teacup is set down with chess-master precision.

'Quite.'

The scene is the payoff to everything Clive built in scenes 29/30 of Episode 2 — the fake letters seeded to American religious press, the American religious press generated genuine outrage, and the genuine outrage is now on television. Philip is watching it as confirmation of the natural order.

The irony is complete: the manufactured reaction has become real, and Philip believes he has simply read it correctly. The operation has succeeded beyond its brief and its architect doesn't know it succeeded at all.
