Cynthia alone. The study, the books, John's margin note in 'The Tao of Physics': 'Everything is connected. Except us.' She closes the book without comment.

The wardrobe. His clothes feeling like costumes. The small box: the white feather, the dried flower, the photograph she wasn't meant to see.

She doesn't react. No tears. No anger. Just a long, quiet breath.

She puts it back. Closes the wardrobe door. The phone: 'Hello, Mimi? It's Cynthia. Would it be alright if Julian and I came to stay for a few days?'

Not fleeing. Closing a door she should have closed a long time ago.

Cynthia has received the verdict. The marriage is intellectually over. Cynthia makes the physical decision. The phone call to Mimi is the action that makes the intellectual decision real. Cynthia has chosen her path. The decision is made without performance, without announcement. The quiet dignity of the scene is its power.
