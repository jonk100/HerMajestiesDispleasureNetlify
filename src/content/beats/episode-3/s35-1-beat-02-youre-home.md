Cynthia: 'Who is she really, John?' John searching for the neat answer. There isn't one.

'Someone who sends me things that make sense when everything else feels plastic.'

Cynthia: 'And I'm what?'

John: 'You're home.' Meant as comfort. It lands like a verdict.

Cynthia: 'Home's not supposed to feel like a cage, John.' She turns to go.

John: 'Neither is the world.' She pauses but doesn't look back. 'Then maybe stop bringing it all in here.'

John names the marriage's honest state without intending to — 'you're home' as verdict rather than comfort. Cynthia names hers: 'home's not supposed to feel like a cage.' Both have told the truth. The marriage cannot hold it.

The verdict has been delivered by accident. Both of them know now. Cynthia exits. John is alone with the reel-to-reel and Yoko's laughter audible between leader tape.
