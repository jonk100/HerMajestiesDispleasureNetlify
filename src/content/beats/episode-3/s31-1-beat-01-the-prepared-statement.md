John looks at the card Brian has placed in front of him — the prepared statement. His jaw tightens. Paul says, 'just read it, mate. Then we can get back to the music.'

John reads it, careful, controlled — then looks up, almost amused, inserting the qualification that wasn't in the statement: 'I was talking about the fact that kids... they go to our shows, not to church.'

He cannot perform retraction without inserting truth. The card is the operation's most complete success — it has forced John into public self-regulation. The press conference is a partial failure of crisis management. John has apologized without retracting. This is the best he can do and it's not enough.
