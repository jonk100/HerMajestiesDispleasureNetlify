The montage pays off to Soho bookshop scene from Episode 2. George in Bombay — kurta, cross-legged floor, the awkward posture.

'You are not a Beatle. You are a student.'

The sitar rests clumsily. 'Do not be sorry. Be patient.'

The Dal Lake houseboat. The simple raga, cleaner and more fluid. 'Maya.' The word landing for George as revelation. The distant screaming fans fade in to replace the sitar.

Three beats compressed into a single structural unit — arrival, instruction, arrival at peace — because compression is correct. George's journey here doesn't need scene-by-scene granularity.

George finds the philosophy. 'Maya' gives a name to the emptiness he's been feeling. He is no longer just a musician — he is a seeker who has found his path. The operation nudged him here but the destination is real. His internal peace is now established — which makes his later withdrawal from the band legible as arrival rather than retreat.
