Nigel opens his desk drawer. The surveillance notebook — old, soft leather, years of jacket pockets. He opens it. He knows the page. He turns to it.

CLOSE ON THE PAGE:
'Subject observed explaining guitar string composition to young son, Julian. Subject states the instrument is capable of "stories about love." — No action required.'

Below, in different ink, added later: 'Yet.'

Nigel looks at the word 'yet.' He looks at it for a long time.

He takes his pen. He crosses it out. Slowly. Deliberately. One line through it.

He closes the notebook. He places it back in the drawer. He closes the drawer.

He looks across at Clive. Clive looks back at him. The wind howls.

FADE TO BLACK.
