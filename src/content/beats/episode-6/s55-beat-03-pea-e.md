Yoko does not see a technical fault. She sees a canvas. She steps forward into the blinding white void. Takes the microphone. And lets out a single, piercing, primal SCREAM. 'A sound that rips through the hall, a bolt of pure, avant-garde energy. It is not a scream of fear. It is a scream of art.' The audience stunned. A few people in the front beginning to applaud uncertainly. In the rafters: Yoko's shriek cuts through Arthur's professional composure. He flinches violently. His finger jerks on the trigger. The shot goes wild. Hits the 'UNICEF: CONCERT FOR PEACE' banner. The 'C' in 'PEACE' rips and flaps down, dangling by a thread. The message now reads: PEA E. Clive staring at the ruined banner: 'The Pigeon has flown the coop... Abort. Abort the composition.' Then: John recovering, stepping back to the microphone, finishing the final chords. The audience erupting in confused but thunderous applause. John and Yoko taking a bow. 'They are alive. They are triumphant. They have no idea what just happened.'
