The echoing hall of civil service indifference. Harsh fluorescent lights, worn linoleum. The queue. John and Yoko in dark coats, blending in. John with a plain cardboard box. At the counter: Brenda — 'a woman who looks as if she were born into her postal worker's uniform.' 'Next, please.' The box slid through. Brenda: 'Contents?' John, quietly: 'A medal.' Brenda's bored tick. 'Right. Needs to go recorded. That'll be one and six.' John pays. V.O.: 'Your Majesty, I am returning this MBE as a protest against Britain's involvement in Nigeria-Biafra thing... against our support of America in Vietnam... and against Cold Turkey slipping down the charts.' The stamp licked with a practiced flick. The rubber stamp brought down with a loud, final, echoing THUD. The box drops into a canvas mail sack. Dull, anticlimactic THUD. Brenda, already looking past them: 'Next.' John and Yoko turn and walk into the anonymous London crowd.
