s-EXT./INT. HIGH PARK FARM, SCOTLAND - DAY (1971)

a-The Scottish hills in morning grey. The farm feels remote and peaceful, as if it exists at the edge of the world. The landscape is rolling, ancient, indifferent to human concerns. The air is clean, cold, carrying the scent of damp earth and distant sheep.

a-Paul McCartney (28) is carrying a guitar from the barn to the farmhouse. He has a beard, wears wellies, looks like a man who has chosen this life. He is not performing - just carrying an instrument, the way a farmer carries tools.

a-He stops on the path, stands in the grey morning air, and plays a few chords. Not a song. Not 'Maybe I'm Amazed' or 'Ram' or anything finished. Just chords.

a-He changes the chord. Listens. Changes it again.

a-He is finding something. The thing he was trying to find when Linda said 'that's a nice one, what is it?' in Episode 5. The answer, here, is the same: 'Don't know yet. It's just... there.'

a-He plays the fragment again. The sound hangs in the Scottish air, clean and pure. He stops. Looks out at the Scottish hills. The morning grey.

a-Then he plays it a third time - slightly differently - and this time he keeps going. He plays for thirty seconds. Not a finished song. A direction. A possibility. The melody has shape but no name, form but no destination.

a-He walks the rest of the way to the farmhouse, still playing, the guitar becoming part of his movement. The music follows him like a companion, like something he's been searching for and finally found.

a-LINDA MCCARTNEY (29) appears at the door with a mug of tea, camera around her neck. She hears the fragment. She doesn't say anything. She just watches him come up the path.

a-There is something in her expression - not pride, not excitement, but quiet recognition. She has heard this before, in fragments, in moments when he thought no one was listening. But this time it's different. This time it's staying.

a-He gets to the door. Stops playing. Takes the tea. The guitar hangs from his shoulder, still humming with the music.

c-PAUL
d-I think I've got one.

a-The words are simple, understated. This is not a declaration of genius. This is a man acknowledging that something has arrived.

c-LINDA
d-(smiling)
d-What's it called?

a-PAUL
d-(thinks)
d-Nothing yet.

a-He goes inside. Linda watches him go, then raises her camera and takes a photograph of the empty path where he was standing.

a-The click of the camera shutter is the only sound. The photograph captures not a person, but the space where music happened. The absence is as important as the presence.

a-Inside the farmhouse, we hear the fragment again, slightly different this time. Paul is working it out, finding the shape. The music continues, growing, changing.

a-Outside, the Scottish hills remain unchanged. The grey morning light begins to shift toward something warmer. The day continues.

a-This is not the end of the story. This is not the resolution of anything. This is just a man finding music again, on the other side of everything that tried to take it away.

a-The melody returns. That is enough.

t-FADE OUT.
