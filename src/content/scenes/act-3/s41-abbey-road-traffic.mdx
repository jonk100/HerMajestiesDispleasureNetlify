s-EXT. ABBEY ROAD – MORNING (AUGUST 1969)

a-STEVEN CONRAD DEADPAN OPENING:

a-A perfect London morning. The light is soft, the air is still. NIGEL FINCH (53) stands on the sidewalk, looking like any other civil servant on his way to another day of bureaucratic drudgery.

a-He holds a radio to his ear. The antenna extends stiffly upward. He speaks into it with the flat, measured tones of a man coordinating road maintenance.

c-NIGEL
d-(into radio)
Control, this is Finch. Do you copy?

c-RADIO VOICE
d-(loud static, then)
Loud and clear, Finch. What's your status?

c-NIGEL
Subjects are in position. We're ready for attempt one. Hold the Volkswagen where it is, Constable. We need the composition... 'controlled.'

a-Across the street, THE BEATLES stand near the zebra crossing. Paul McCartney is barefoot. John Lennon looks like he hasn't slept. Ringo Starr is impeccably dressed. George Harrison just looks tired.

a-A young POLICE CONSTABLE (20s) stands next to a Volkswagen Beetle, looking confused.

c-CONSTABLE
d-(into his own radio)
Sir, with respect, they're just walking across the road. Over and over.

a-Nigel lowers his radio. He walks over to the Constable, moving with the unhurried pace of a man who has all day.

c-NIGEL
Constable, that vehicle is precisely where it needs to be.

c-CONSTABLE
But sir, it's causing a backup. Three cars already.

c-NIGEL
d-(adjusts his spectacles)
Those cars are part of the controlled environment. We need them there.

c-CONSTABLE
Part of what, sir?

c-NIGEL
d-(pauses, chooses his words carefully)
Part of the... urban composition study. Ministry of Transport is documenting pedestrian flow patterns in relation to static vehicular obstacles.

a-The Constable stares at him.

c-CONSTABLE
The Ministry of Transport is studying The Beatles crossing the road?

c-NIGEL
The Ministry of Transport is studying pedestrian flow patterns, Constable. The identity of the pedestrians is irrelevant to the data collection.

a-Across the street, the Beatles attempt another crossing. Iain Macmillan, the photographer, stands on a ladder in the middle of the road.

c-MACMILLAN
Right, one more time! Walk briskly but naturally!

a-They start walking. A taxi tries to get through. The Constable holds up his hand.

c-TAXI DRIVER
d-(shouting)
I've got a fare! They're just walking across the road!

c-NIGEL
d-(into radio)
Control, we have an unauthorized vehicle attempting to breach the perimeter. Advise.

c-RADIO VOICE
Perimeter? Finch, what perimeter? You're supposed to be coordinating street cleaning for the cultural delegation visit.

c-NIGEL
d-(into radio, calmly)
The street cleaning is proceeding as planned. The perimeter is to ensure... cleaning efficiency.

c-CONSTABLE
d-(to Nigel)
Sir, the photographer is getting impatient. And I think John Lennon just flipped off the taxi driver.

c-NIGEL
d-(nods)
Noted. Standard celebrity behavior. Document and continue.

a-Nigel watches as the Beatles line up for another attempt. They walk across the zebra crossing. Macmillan clicks rapidly. This time, it works. The composition is perfect.

c-MACMILLAN
Got it! That's the one!

a-The Beatles start dispersing. Paul McCartney heads back toward the studio. John Lennon lights a cigarette. George Harrison gets into a waiting car.

a-Nigel speaks into his radio.

c-NIGEL
Control, primary objective achieved. Commencing phase two.

c-RADIO VOICE
Phase two of what, Finch? The street sweeping?

c-NIGEL
d-(into radio)
The street sweeping, yes. The area needs to be cleared of all operational debris.

a-Nigel walks to the zebra crossing. He bends down, picks up something tiny from the pavement. It's a cigarette butt. He places it in an evidence bag.

c-CONSTABLE
d-(watching him)
Sir, is that... evidence?

c-NIGEL
Without a context, Constable, it's just litter. With a context, it's potentially compromising material.

c-CONSTABLE
Compromising what, sir?

a-Nigel looks at the cigarette butt in the bag. He looks at the studio where the Beatles disappeared. He looks at the zebra crossing where history was just made.

c-NIGEL
Compromising everything, Constable. Everything.

a-He places the bag in his pocket. He straightens his jacket. He looks like what he is: a middle-aged civil servant who has just spent three hours coordinating traffic for a photograph, while secretly making sure no evidence of a five-year covert operation remains on the pavement.

c-CONSTABLE
Well, sir. Mission accomplished, I suppose.

c-NIGEL
d-(nods slowly)
Yes, Constable. Mission accomplished.

a-He starts walking away, moving with the same measured pace he arrived with. He doesn't look back. He doesn't need to. The job is done. The photograph is taken. The evidence is collected. The operation is one step closer to its conclusion.

c-CONSTABLE
d-(calling after him)
Sir! What about the Volkswagen?

a-Nigel doesn't break stride. He speaks into his radio without turning around.

c-NIGEL
d-(into radio)
Control, abandon the Volkswagen. It's served its purpose.

c-RADIO VOICE
What purpose, Finch? Finch, what purpose?

a-Nigel keeps walking. The radio crackles with unanswered questions. The Constable stands with the Volkswagen. The traffic begins to move again. Life goes on.

c-NIGEL
d-(to himself, so quietly no one can hear)
The purpose was to control the composition. The purpose was always to control the composition.

t-FADE OUT.
