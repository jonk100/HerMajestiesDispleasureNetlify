t-FADE IN:

s-INT. MI5 REGISTRY - DAY (FEBRUARY 1970)

a-The registry. Filing cabinets line the walls like soldiers at attention. The air smells of old paper and institutional dust. A single bare bulb hangs from the ceiling, casting everything in a yellowish, bureaucratic glow.

a-PERCY (62) is alone. Nigel and Clive have left with the Anschütz rifle case, their footsteps still echoing in the corridor. Percy is performing the final administrative task of the day: filing the equipment sign-out form.

a-Percy opens the operational subject files cabinet. The drawer slides open with a sound like a coffin lid, revealing rows of manila folders organized with military precision. Each label is typed, centered, as if the spacing itself matters more than the contents.

a-He works with the methodical rhythm of a man who has performed this ritual a thousand times. His movements are economical - no wasted motion. The pen clicks against the form with the steady beat of a metronome that has measured his career.

a-He pulls LENNON's file. The weight is immediately apparent - the folder bulges with five years of accumulated surveillance, psychological assessments, field notes. The paper edges are soft from constant handling. This file has physical presence.

a-Percy opens it. The scent of stale paranoia rises from the pages. He scans the first report - Episode 1 briefing assessment, typed on official letterhead, the ink slightly faded after five years but the authority still absolute.

a-CLOSE ON THE PAGE: 'Subject displays a baseline level of contentment impervious to psycho-chemical manipulation. Recommend: ignore.' Signed FINCH.

a-Percy's fingers trace the signature. He remembers that briefing - Finch's precise, clinical assessment of the four Beatles, how he dismissed Starr as irrelevant before even meeting them. The memory is sharp, clear.

a-He pulls MCCARTNEY's file. Substantial but lighter than Lennon's. The label shows establishment grooming - diplomatic pressure, honors, the knighthood campaign. Percy remembers the paperwork required for each new initiative.

a-He pulls HARRISON's file. A solid folder, respectable weight. The tab indicates spiritual withdrawal - Ravi Shankar, meditation, the gradual drift from the band. Percy recalls the complexity of monitoring a man who was physically present but emotionally absent.

a-He pulls STARR's file.

a-It is almost entirely empty. Three sheets of paper, floating in the drawer like forgotten documents.

a-Percy examines the three sheets with the intensity of a forensic accountant. The first: the original Episode 1 briefing assessment. He reads it again, the words carrying the same clinical certainty they did five years ago.

a-CLOSE ON THE PAGE: 'Recommend: ignore.'

a-The second sheet: the single field report from Episode 3. Percy remembers Barrow's frustration - having to file something when nothing happened. The handwriting is rushed, as if the agent couldn't believe this was worth the ink.

a-CLOSE ON THE PAGE: 'No Significant Activity to Report.'

a-Percy runs a finger over the pre-printed dates spanning 1965-1969. Perfect bureaucratic precision. Each date filled in with same mechanical regularity. Filed by... nobody. The emptiness of this is the most damning evidence of all.

a-Percy looks at the three sheets. He looks at the three thick files on his desk. He looks back at the thin one. His face shows no surprise. No confusion. Just the quiet, professional acceptance of a man who has seen bureaucratic absurdity in all its forms, but this time there's something else - a flicker of understanding, perhaps even admiration.

a-This is not a discovery. This is a confirmation. This is the institution admitting, through its own perfect records, that it was wrong about Starr from the beginning. That Finch's assessment was correct. That years of surveillance were wasted. That Barrow's frustration was justified.

a-He carefully replaces the three sheets in the Starr file and closes it. The sound is final, definitive. He places it back in the cabinet. The drawer slides shut with the same coffin-like sound.

a-He picks up his landmine from the corner of the room. The metal is cold, heavy. He resumes polishing, the rhythmic scraping filling the silence. But now the rhythm is different - more thoughtful, less mechanical. The landmine is easier to understand than the files, but the files are easier to understand than the people they contain.

a-Outside the window, London continues its business. Inside, the bureaucracy has confirmed what it knew all along.

t-FADE OUT.
