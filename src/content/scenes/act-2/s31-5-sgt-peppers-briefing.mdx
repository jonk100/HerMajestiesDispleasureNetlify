s-INT. BUCKINGHAM PALACE CONFERENCE ROOM – DAY (1967)

a-A room of dark wood paneling and institutional silence. A massive oak table dominates, polished to a mirror finish. On one wall hangs a portrait of Queen Victoria, looking distinctly unimpressed.

a-SIR DEREK FINCH-AMORY (60s), a man whose face is permanently fixed in a state of mild outrage, paces back and forth. He carries a wooden pointer like it's a weapon of war.

a-NIGEL FINCH (50) and CLIVE BARROW (43) sit opposite each other. Nigel looks like he's preparing his own execution. Clive looks like he's about to be given a field promotion.

a-On an easel stands the Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band album cover. It looks absurdly vibrant in the sepia-toned room.

c-SIR DEREK
d-(slams pointer on table)
Right. Let's talk about this kaleidoscopic clusterfuck, shall we?

a-He gestures wildly at the album cover with the pointer.

c-SIR DEREK
Nigel! Clive! Explain yourselves! Why is there a Shirley Temple doll wearing a sweater that says 'Welcome the Rolling Stones'? Is that a threat? Is it an invitation to a coup?

a-Nigel opens his mouth. Sir Derek cuts him off.

c-SIR DEREK
Don't answer that! It was rhetorical! The answer is obviously yes! It's both! It's a threat-coup!

a-Clive leans forward, eyes gleaming with what he believes is profound insight.

c-CLIVE
If I may, sir? I believe we're witnessing what I call 'cultural insurgency through semiotic overload.' The doll represents...

c-SIR DEREK
d-(points pointer at Clive like a gun)
Shut up, Barrow! You've done quite enough damage already!

a-He turns back to the album cover, jabbing the pointer at various figures.

c-SIR DEREK
Look at this! Bob Dylan! Karl Marx! Laurel and Hardy! It's a who's who of subversion! And this...

d-(pointer stabs at Aleister Crowley's face)
THIS! Aleister Crowley! The Great Beast! Wearing sunglasses! Like he's waiting for a taxi to Hades!

a-Nigel sighs quietly. It's barely audible, but in the room's silence, it echoes like a gunshot.

c-SIR DEREK
d-(whips around to face Nigel)
Something amusing, Finch? Something funny about the occultist drug addict being featured on what children are buying for their pocket money?

c-NIGEL
No, sir. Just... thinking about the weather.

c-SIR DEREK
The weather! The weather is fine, Finch! What's not fine is the complete collapse of Western civilization happening on a 12-inch cardboard square!

a-He paces again, pointer tapping rhythmically against his palm.

c-SIR DEREK
And the 'Paul is Dead' clues! Don't think I haven't seen the intelligence reports on that! The 'walrus' reference! The 'turn me on, dead man' backward masking! Those aren't fan theories, you idiots!

a-He stops, leans across the table, voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper.

c-SIR DEREK
Those are leaked signaling codes. Accidentally leaked signaling codes that YOU TWO let slip during your... what did you call it, Barrow? Your 'aesthetic penetration protocols'?

a-Clive sits up straighter, puffing his chest out slightly.

c-CLIVE
The Klaus methodology has been quite successful, sir. We've established...

c-SIR DEREK
d-(interrupts, voice rising)
You established a German art critic who thinks lamp posts are metaphors! You spent six months trying to influence album cover design with drawings of sad fountains!

c-NIGEL
d-(muttering)
To be fair, the fountain was rather well-executed.

a-Sir Derek's head snaps toward Nigel. For a moment, there's silence. Then Sir Derek's face breaks into something that might be a smile, if smiles could cause frostbite.

c-SIR DEREK
Well, well. The comedian speaks. Tell me, Finch, do you find this funny?

d-(pointer sweeps across the album cover)
Do you find the systematic dismantling of everything this country stands for amusing? The Beatles aren't just making music anymore! They're creating a new reality! A reality where Marx plays bass and Crowley handles percussion!

a-He walks to the window, looks out at the perfectly manicured palace gardens.

c-SIR DEREK
The Queen asked me this morning if I thought 'Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds' was about her granddaughter. I had to lie. I had to say it was about a drawing. We both know it's about LSD. We both know it's about turning the next generation into psychedelic zombies who will hand over the keys to the kingdom for a sitar solo.

a-He turns back, pointer now aimed directly between Nigel's eyes.

c-SIR DEREK
So here's what's going to happen. Operation Minuet is escalating. We're moving from psychological warfare to cultural counter-insurgency. You two will not just observe. You will intervene. You will neutralize. You will understand?

c-CLIVE
d-(beaming)
Cultural counter-insurgency! I've been developing theories about that! The application of...

c-SIR DEREK
d-(interrupts)
Barrow, if you use the phrase 'aesthetic penetration' one more time, I will personally see to it that your next posting is in a basement room where the only cultural influence you'll have is the sound of your own sanity slowly draining away.

a-Clive's smile falters slightly.

c-NIGEL
Sir, with respect, what exactly does 'intervene' mean in practical terms?

a-Sir Derek smiles. It's not a pleasant sight.

c-SIR DEREK
It means you stop being observers and start being active participants in the defense of the realm. It means you find out what they're planning next. It means you prevent it.

a-He walks to the easel, studies the album cover one more time.

c-SIR DEREK
And for God's sake, find out why there's a hand coming out of Paul McCartney's head holding a garden gnome. That can't be good for anyone.

t-FADE OUT.
