s-INT. BASEMENT SURVEILLANCE ROOM – NIGHT (1968)

a-A cramped, windowless basement that smells of damp concrete and old electronics. The only light comes from:

a-A single bare bulb hanging from the ceiling
a-The warm orange glow of vacuum tubes in audio equipment
a-The faint green luminescence of reel-to-reel tape decks

a-Two reel-to-reel machines sit on a metal table. Their tape reels spin slowly, hypnotically. The sound filling the room is the chaotic, layered nightmare of "Revolution 9."

a-NIGEL FINCH (52) sits in a wooden chair that has seen better decades. He stares at the opposite wall, his face illuminated by the flickering tube lights. He hasn't moved in twenty minutes.

a-CLIVE BARROW (45) sits at a small desk, furiously writing notes on a legal pad. He's surrounded by charts, graphs, and what appears to be a hand-drawn map of the North Sea.

c-CLIVE
d-(excitedly)
There! Did you hear it? The "Number 9" loop! It's not just a loop, Nigel! It's coordinates!

a-Nigel doesn't respond. His eyes are fixed on the wall as if he's trying to see through it.

c-CLIVE
d-(holding up his notepad)
59 degrees north by 2 degrees west! That's not random! That's exactly where a Soviet submarine would surface to receive instructions! The Beatles are giving the KGB GPS coordinates through avant-garde tape collage!

a-The tape continues its assault: distorted voices, orchestral crashes, football stadium announcers, the endless repetition of "Number nine... number nine... number nine..."

c-CLIVE
And the football crowd! "Hold that line!" That's obviously a reference to holding the line against capitalist oppression! The crowd noise isn't crowd noise—it's the sound of the proletariat rising!

a-Nigel slowly blinks. It's the only movement he's made since the scene began.

c-CLIVE
I've been cataloguing the audio layers. There are at least seventeen distinct subliminal messages here. The "turn me on, dead man" backwards masking, the string section that sounds suspiciously like the Soviet national anthem played at half speed, the...

a-He stops, noticing Nigel's complete lack of response.

c-CLIVE
Nigel? Are you getting any of this? This is brilliant! They're using sound collage as encryption! It's what I call 'sonic steganography'!

a-Nigel's eyes slowly drift toward the spinning tape reels. He watches them turn, round and round, like some terrible carousel.

c-NIGEL
d-(voice dry as dust)
It's a tape, Clive. It's sound on tape.

c-CLIVE
d-(waving his notepad dismissively)
That's what they WANT you to think! That's the beauty of it! The surface-level interpretation masks the deeper meaning! The chaos is actually order! The noise is actually signal!

a-The "Number nine" loop repeats again. And again. And again.

c-NIGEL
d-(still staring at the wall)
How long have we been listening to this?

c-CLIVE
d-(checks his watch)
Six hours. But I've isolated the key patterns! The "Number 9" loop appears exactly 89 times. 89! That's not random! That's the atomic number of actinium! They're signaling about nuclear capabilities!

a-Nigel slowly turns his head to look at Clive. His expression is one of profound, soul-crushing weariness.

c-NIGEL
Actinium. The Beatles are sending secret messages about actinium.

c-CLIVE
d-(nodding seriously)
Through a German art critic who works as a coat check girl at a London club! It's a double-blind! Triple-agent! Quadruple-redundancy!

a-The tape reels continue spinning. The vacuum tubes glow their warm, steady orange. The "Number nine" loop repeats.

c-NIGEL
I used to like music, you know.

c-CLIVE
d-(distracted, drawing on his map)
Of course you did! Everyone likes music! That's how they get you! That's the vector! The emotional payload delivery system!

a-Nigel leans forward, elbows on his knees. He puts his face in his hands.

c-NIGEL
I had a life before this. I had roses. Prize-winning roses. They need watering. They need pruning. They don't... they don't repeat the same phrase over and over again until you forget your own name.

c-CLIVE
d-(not really listening)
The repetition is the key! It's like Morse code but for the subconscious! Nine... nine... nine... it's conditioning! Behavioral modification through sonic imprinting!

a-Nigel looks up from his hands. His eyes land on the reel-to-reel machines. He watches the tape spin, round and round, hypnotically, endlessly.

c-NIGEL
d-(softly, to himself)
It never stops.

c-CLIVE
Exactly! That's the point! It's designed to never stop! It's psychological warfare! Cultural insurgency! Sonic...

a-He stops, looks at Nigel properly for the first time in hours.

c-CLIVE
Are you feeling alright, Nigel? You look a bit... pale.

c-NIGEL
d-(stares at Clive with dead eyes)
The residue.

c-CLIVE
What's that?

c-NIGEL
d-(louder, looking at the wall)
The residue. That's what you called it. The effect left behind. You were right. It's real.

a-He stands up slowly, like an old man. Walks to the wall. Places his palm flat against the concrete.

c-NIGEL
I can feel it. In the walls. In my teeth. In the space behind my eyes.

c-CLIVE
d-(concerned)
Nigel, maybe we should take a break. Get some fresh air.

c-NIGEL
d-(muffled against the wall)
There is no fresh air. There's only this room. And this sound. And the number nine.

a-He turns back to the wall, presses his forehead against the cool concrete.

c-NIGEL
Just... just tell me one thing, Clive.

c-CLIVE
Anything, old chap.

c-NIGEL
d-(muffled)
When this is all over... do you think we'll be able to hear silence again?

a-The tape reels spin. The vacuum tubes glow. The "Number nine" loop repeats.

a-Clive looks at Nigel, then at his charts, then back at Nigel. For the first time, a flicker of something other than excitement crosses his face. It might be concern. It might be confusion. It might be the dawning realization that his partner is slowly being driven mad by sound collage.

c-CLIVE
d-(uncertainly)
I... I'll make a note of that. In the report.

t-FADE OUT.
