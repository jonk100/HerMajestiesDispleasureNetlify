t-FADE IN:

s-INT. CHICAGO HOTEL PRESS ROOM  DAY (AUGUST 1966)

a-A low-ceilinged banquet room repurposed as a PRESS CONFERENCE. Folding chairs, bad carpeting, sweating microphones.

a-At the front table sit THE BEATLES:

a-JOHN, pale but composed behind his round glasses.

a-PAUL, wearing his professional half-smile.

a-GEORGE, withdrawn.

a-RINGO, trying to look encouraging.

a-BRIAN EPSTEIN stands to the side in an immaculate suit, fingers white around a stack of notes.

a-The room is PACKED with AMERICAN REPORTERS, mostly middle-aged white men in short-sleeved shirts. Flashbulbs POP.

c-REPORTER #1
p-(calling out)
 d-Mr. Lennon, do you still say you're more popular than Jesus Christ?

a-Murmurs. Pens poised.

c-JOHN
p-(flat, controlled)
 d-I never said I was better or greater than Jesus. I said we were more popular with the kids. Which, looking round, seems to be true.

a-The room TIGHTENS.

c-REPORTER #2
p-(pushy)
 d-Do you think that was an appropriate thing to say?

c-BRIAN
p-(cutting in, smooth)
 d-If I may

c-REPORTER #2
 d-We'd like to hear from Mr. Lennon.

a-Brian swallows it. Sits. This is not his room.

a-John glances down at a CARD Brian has placed in front of him. A prepared statement. His jaw tightens.

c-PAUL
p-(low, to John)
 d-Just read it, mate. Then we can get back to the music.

a-John looks out at the sea of EXPECTANT FACES.

c-JOHN
p-(reading, careful)
 d-I'm not anti-Christ or anti-religion or anti-God.
 p-(beat)
 d-I'm not saying we're better or greater.
 p-(looks up, almost amused)
 d-I was talking about the fact that kids... they go to our shows, not to church.

a-A REPORTER scribbles: "NOT ANTI-GOD" and underlines it twice.

c-REPORTER #3
p-(shouting)
 d-So you admit it was wrong?

a-John exhales.

c-JOHN
p-(choosing his words)
 d-I said what I said... and it was taken wrong.
 p-(shrugs)
 d-And now it's all this.

a-A ripple of TYPING and FLASHES. They have their quote.

s-ANGLE ON BRIAN

a-He watches, immaculate mask cracking at the edges. This is the nightmare: his "boys" cornered, the brand burning in effigy outside.

c-BRIAN
p-(to the room, urgent charm)
 d-The point is the boys are terribly sorry for any offense caused. Theyre entertainers, not theologians.

c-REPORTER #4
p-(dry)
 d-They seem to be doing both.

s-ON PAUL

a-He leans toward his microphone, doing the thing he always does: try to make it all sound reasonable.

c-PAUL
p-(open, conciliatory)
 d-We love the States. Always have. We don't want to upset anybody. It's just John talking in England how he talks. Different place. Different sort of... humour.

a-He offers a small, disarming smile.

a-Some of the room softens. Not much.

s-ON GEORGE AND RINGO

a-GEORGE stares at the tablecloth, thumb worrying a cigarette burn.

c-GEORGE
p-(quiet, mostly to himself)
 d-It's all maya anyway.

a-RINGO leans into his mic with simple sincerity.

c-RINGO
 d-We're just a band, you know.

a-This accidentally gets a LAUGH. The tension releases for a beat.

s-BACK TO JOHN

a-He hears the laugh. It doesn't reach him.

a-He looks out at the forest of hands, the shouting mouths, the flashes. The "more popular than Jesus" comment had been a line in a quiet English paper. Here, its a TRIAL.

c-REPORTER #1
p-(pressing)
 d-Would you say something like that again, Mr. Lennon?

a-Beat.

c-JOHN
p-(after a long pause)
 d-Not to you.

a-A NERVOUS LAUGH. Brian winces.

c-BRIAN
p-(trying to close)
 d-Thank you, gentlemen, that will be all.

a-He gestures to the aides. The conference is OVER.

s-INT. HOTEL CORRIDOR  MOMENTS LATER

a-The Beatles spill out of the press room into a beige corridor. Muffled SHOUTS and CAMERA FLASHES chase them.

a-Brian closes the door on the noise, leaning against it for a second.

c-BRIAN
p-(to John, low)
 d-Youve no idea what it took to get them to even offer you that platform.

c-JOHN
p-(tired)
 d-I just told the truth in the first place.

c-PAUL
p-(gently)
 d-Yeah, but now weve got to live with it.

a-A beat.

c-GEORGE
 d-Do we have to keep doing this?

c-RINGO
 d-The press bits? Or the being shot at?

a-They look at him. None of them laughs this time.

a-Down the corridor, a HOTEL TV in a bar shows B-ROLL of TEENAGERS burning records in a parking lot. The sound is low, but the IMAGES flicker over John's face.

c-JOHN
p-(barely audible)
 d-It's a living.

a-He walks away toward the lift, smaller than the posters outside.

a-Behind him, Brian straightens his tie, already rehearsing the next phone call, the next apology.

a-Paul watches John go, caught between following his friend and smoothing things over with the man who keeps the circus running.

c-PAUL
p-(to Brian)
 d-We can't keep touring like this.

a-Brian doesn't answer. He doesn't have an answer.

a-The LIFT DOORS close on John's reflection.
