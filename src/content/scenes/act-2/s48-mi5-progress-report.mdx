t-FADE IN:

s-INT. BUCKINGHAM PALACE STUDY - DAY (1968)

a-The masculine sanctuary. PHILIP looks impatient. MOUNTBATTEN sits with military patience. NIGEL stands rigidly. CLIVE hovers with barely contained excitement.

a-Philip gestures to the space in front of his desk like a conductor.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-Report.

c-NIGEL
p-(precise)
d-Sir. Operation Minuet has achieved measurable destabilization across multiple target vectors.

c-CLIVE
p-(jumping in)
d-Subject Lennon shows increased paranoia and ideological radicalization. Subject McCartney displays growing affinity for establishment validation. Subject Harrison has effectively withdrawn into spiritual isolation. Subject Starr remains... inexplicably content.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-Content?

c-CLIVE
d-Subject immunity, Your Royal Highness. A statistical anomaly. We’re developing countermeasures.

a-Philip exchanges a look with Mountbatten that says: “Countermeasures? For happiness?”

c-MOUNTBATTEN
d-And the band’s commercial viability?

c-NIGEL
d-Intact, sir. Record sales remain strong. Touring has ceased, but that appears to be a strategic choice rather than a consequence of our operations.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-So they’re still famous. Still rich. Still... everywhere.

c-CLIVE
d-But they’re fragmented! That’s the key. The unit cohesion has been compromised by at least forty-seven percent.

c-NIGEL
p-(quietly)
d-I believe you invented that metric, Clive.

c-CLIVE
d-It’s a psychological metric! Based on observed behavioral patterns and communication frequency analysis.

a-Mountbatten steeples his fingers, considering.

c-MOUNTBATTEN
d-And the long-term prognosis?

c-CLIVE
d-Total dissolution within eighteen months. Mark my words.

c-NIGEL
d-Or they might make another album.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-I don’t pay for albums, Finch. I pay for results.

a-He stands, walks to the window. Looks out at the palace gardens.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-This was supposed to be simple. Apply pressure. Watch them break. Instead, they’re just... becoming stranger versions of themselves.

c-CLIVE
d-But that’s exactly it! The strangeness is the success! They’re alienating their base! They’re—

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-Enough.

a-He turns back, his expression cold.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-Escalate.

c-NIGEL
p-(carefully)
d-Sir?

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-You heard me. Escalate. I want this finished before the decade is out.

a-Mountbatten raises an eyebrow but doesn’t object.

c-MOUNTBATTEN
d-We’ll explore alternative operational parameters.

c-CLIVE
d-(gleeful)
d-I have several theories about sonic disruption and cognitive fragmentation!

a-Nigel closes his eyes briefly. The same expression a man makes when he realizes he’s going to have to explain why the government is trying to use sound waves to break up a pop band.

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-See that you do.

a-Philip dismisses them with a wave. Clive practically bounces out. Nigel follows with the measured steps of a man walking to his own execution.

a-Mountbatten remains, studying Philip.

c-MOUNTBATTEN
d-You’re enjoying this, aren’t you?

c-PRINCE PHILIP
d-Not enjoying. No. But there’s a certain satisfaction in watching the world remember its place.

a-Outside the window, London continues its business, completely unaware that the British government is about to declare cultural war on four musicians who mostly want to be left alone.

t-FADE OUT.
