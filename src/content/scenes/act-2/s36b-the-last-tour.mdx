t-FADE IN:

s-INT. CANDLESTICK PARK DRESSING ROOM - DAY (AUGUST 29, 1966)

a-A bleak, concrete room. Metal lockers, fluorescent lights, the smell of stale sweat and disinfectant.

a-THE BEATLES sit in mismatched chairs, still in their stage clothes. The distant ROAR of the crowd filters through the walls—muted, like a memory.

a-JOHN stares at the ceiling, counting ceiling tiles.

a-PAUL polishes his bass with unnecessary intensity.

a-GEORGE rolls a cigarette, his hands steady.

a-RINGO sips water, looking at nothing in particular.

c-PAUL
 d-That’s it, then.

a-No one answers.

c-PAUL
 d-Thirty minutes. They didn’t even hear half of it.

c-GEORGE
 d-They weren’t there to hear.

c-RINGO
 d-They were there to scream.

a-John lowers his head, looks at the others.

c-JOHN
 d-I’m done.

a-Paul stops polishing.

c-PAUL
 d-With touring? Or—

c-JOHN
 d-With this. With pretending we’re a band in a circus tent.

c-GEORGE
 d-We can still make records.

c-JOHN
 d-We can make art.

a-The word hangs in the air. Not just songs. Art.

c-PAUL
 d-And what about the fans? The people who paid?

c-JOHN
 d-They’ll buy the records. They’ll come to the shows when we’re ready. When it’s not... this.

a-He gestures vaguely at the walls, at the noise outside.

c-RINGO
 d-I wouldn’t mind not having to get on another plane for a while.

c-GEORGE
 d-Or another stage with a fence between us and them.

a-Paul looks at his bass, then at John. A long moment of calculation. Then he nods. Slowly.

c-PAUL
 d-Right. No more tours.

a-They sit in silence. The decision feels both monumental and inevitable.

c-JOHN
 d-We’ll be a studio band. Like... proper musicians.

c-PAUL
 d-Like we always should have been.

a-George lights his cigarette. The first drag is slow, satisfied.

c-GEORGE
 d-Maya.

a-John and Paul look at him.

c-GEORGE
 d-It’s all illusion anyway. This whole thing. The screaming. The fences. The... Beatles.

a-Ringo smiles faintly.

c-RINGO
 d-Still better than a real job.

a-A small, genuine laugh breaks the tension. They’re still them. Just not on a stage.

c-JOHN
 d-To the studio, then.

c-PAUL
 d-To the studio.

a-They stand, gather their things. The roar outside continues, but it already sounds like another life.

a-They walk out of the dressing room together, not as conquerors, but as survivors choosing a different battlefield.

t-FADE OUT.
