s-INT. ANIMATION STUDIO BASEMENT – NIGHT (1969)

a-TIME JUMP MONTAGE - BETWEEN EPISODES 4 AND 5

a-A single worktable dominates the frame. It's perfectly organized in its chaos:

a-Center: A light table glowing with soft white light
a-Left side: Stacks of animation cels, neatly piled
a-Right side: Magnifying glasses, tiny brushes, pots of paint
a-Top: A row of completed Blue Meanie cels
a-Bottom: A row of incomplete Blue Meanie cels

a-CLIVE BARROW (46) sits hunched over the light table. He wears a green eyeshade like a 1940s accountant. His beret sits beside him, like a retired colleague.

a-He works with the intensity of a watchmaker and the delusion of a prophet.

a-CLOSE UP - CLIVE'S HANDS:

a-They're surprisingly steady. He dips a brush into a tiny pot of red paint. The brush tip is finer than a human hair.

a-CLOSE UP - ANIMATION CEL:

a-A Blue Meanie's face, rendered in bold strokes. Clive carefully paints a microscopic Union Jack flag on one of the Meanie's teeth. It's so small you'd need a magnifying glass to see it.

c-CLIVE
p-(muttering to himself)
d-Subliminal reinforcement. Patriotic conditioning through cartoon villainy. Brilliant.

a-He sets the completed cel aside, picks up another. This one shows the Blue Meanie's hand. Clive paints a tiny crown onto the Meanie's pinky finger.

c-CLIVE
The monarchy in miniature. The crown in the claw. They'll see it. The children will see it, even if they don't know they're seeing it. Their subconscious minds will register the symbols. Their little brains will think: "Perhaps these colorful villains aren't so different from our own beloved institutions after all."

a-WIDE SHOT - COEN BROTHERS FUTILITY:

a-The camera pulls back to reveal the true scale of Clive's delusion. The worktable is surrounded by shelves. And on those shelves are boxes. And in those boxes are thousands of animation cels.

a-Thousands of Blue Meanies. Thousands of Beatles. Thousands of Jeremy Hillary Boobs, Ph.D.

a-Clive has been working for months. He has completed perhaps two dozen cels.

c-CLIVE
p-(nodding with determination)
d-At this rate, I should be finished by... 1987. Plenty of time before the next general election.

a-MONTAGE SEQUENCE:

a-QUICK CUTS:

a-Clive working by day, light table glowing
a-Clive working by night, light table glowing
a-Clive falling asleep at the table, face down on a half-painted Meanie
a-Clive waking up, brush still in hand, continuing immediately
a-Clive talking to the cels, explaining his theories to unresponsive cartoon characters
a-Clive measuring the Union Jack flags with a ruler, ensuring consistency
a-Clive testing different shades of red for the flags, muttering about "proper heraldic colors"

a-TIME LAPSE:

a-The seasons change outside the basement window. We see leaves fall, snow accumulate, flowers bloom. Clive remains at his table, painting microscopic symbols on cartoon villains.

a-VOICEOVER - CLIVE (REPORTING TO SIR DEREK):

c-CLIVE (V.O.)
d-Progress is steady, sir. The visual conditioning protocols are being implemented at the molecular level. Each Blue Meanie now carries between three and seven subliminal patriotic markers. The children won't know why they suddenly feel a surge of loyalty to the Crown when watching the film, but they will feel it. Oh, they will feel it.

a-BACK TO PRESENT:

a-Clive holds up his latest work. A Blue Meanie's nose. On the tip, he has painted a tiny portrait of Queen Elizabeth. It's actually quite good, if you ignore the fact that it's the size of a pinhead and painted on a cartoon villain's nostril.

c-CLIVE
d-Her Majesty, even in the most unexpected places. The ultimate symbol of continuity. The face that launched a thousand ships... or at least a thousand subconscious loyalty triggers.

a-He hears footsteps upstairs. Quickly, he covers his work with a sheet, turns off the light table, and assumes the pose of a man who has just been casually browsing animation cels for entirely legitimate government reasons.

a-The footsteps pass. Clive relaxes, turns the light table back on.

a-He looks at his "done" pile: 24 cels.

a-He looks at his "to do" pile: still approximately 2,977 cels.

c-CLIVE
p-(sighs contentedly)
d-The work of a patriot is never done.

a-He picks up another cel. Another Blue Meanie. Another tiny Union Jack waiting to be painted on another tiny tooth.

c-CLIVE
p-(smiling)
d-One tooth at a time. One flag at a time. One child's subconscious mind at a time.

t-FADE TO BLACK.

t-TITLE CARD: "SIX MONTHS LATER"

t-FADE IN:

a-Same basement. Same table. Same Clive.

a-His "done" pile now contains 47 cels.

a-His "to do" pile still contains approximately 2,953 cels.

c-CLIVE
p-(holding up his latest work)
d-Brilliant. This time I've managed to paint a miniature battleship on the Meanie's eyebrow. The Navy will be pleased.

a-He continues painting. The endless, futile, magnificent work of a man who has completely lost touch with reality but found his true calling.

t-FADE OUT.
