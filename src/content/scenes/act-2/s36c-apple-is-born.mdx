t-FADE IN:

s-INT. LONDON LAW OFFICE - DAY (MAY 1967)

a-A stuffy, wood-paneled room. Leather-bound books, heavy desks. The air smells of old paper and privilege.

a-THE BEATLES sit in expensive but ill-fitting suits. They look like boys playing dress-up in their fathers’ clothes.

a-Across from them, a SOLICITOR (50s, dry, precise) pushes a thick document across the desk.

c-SOLICITOR
 d-Apple Corps Limited. Articles of incorporation. If you’ll just initial here... and here... and sign at the bottom.

a-John grabs a pen first, grinning.

c-JOHN
 d-Right. Our own company. No more managers telling us what to do.

c-PAUL
 d-Except us telling us what to do.

c-GEORGE
 d-Which is probably worse.

c-RINGO
 d-Do I get a parking space?

a-The solicitor doesn’t blink.

c-SOLICITOR
 d-Mr. Starr, you will be a director. You may have whatever parking spaces the company can afford.

c-RINGO
 d-Brilliant.

a-They sign. John with a flourish, Paul with care, George with quiet focus, Ringo with a relieved smile.

c-JOHN
 d-We’re our own bosses now. No more Epstein running everything. No more...

a-He trails off. A flicker of something—guilt? Uncertainty?

c-PAUL
 d-We’ll still talk to Brian. Of course. It’s just... we control the music. And the films. And the... whatever else we want to do.

c-GEORGE
 d-We could release sitar records. No one could stop us.

c-JOHN
 d-We could give money to weird artists. Like Yoko.

a-Paul’s smile tightens for half a second.

c-PAUL
 d-We could invest properly. Not like the merchandising.

c-SOLICITOR
 d-(without looking up)
 d-Indeed. The North American merchandising arrangement remains... suboptimal.

a-Beatles exchange glances. They’d almost forgotten.

c-PAUL
 d-Right. Well. We’ll fix that. We’ll fix everything.

a-They stand, shaking hands with the solicitor. Formal. Businesslike. Completely at odds with who they are.

c-JOHN
 d-This is it, then. The beginning of something new.

c-PAUL
 d-The beginning of us.

s-EXT. LAW OFFICE - CONTINUOUS

a-The Beatles spill out onto the London street, blinking in the sunlight. They look giddy, liberated.

c-JOHN
 d-First thing we do: sign a band that sounds like three cats in a bag.

c-GEORGE
 d-Second thing: release an album of silence.

c-RINGO
 d-Third thing: lunch.

a-They laugh. Real, unguarded laughter.

s-INT. MI5 SURVEILLANCE VAN - SAME TIME

a-NIGEL FINCH watches on a small monitor. Clive Barrow beside him, taking notes.

c-CLIVE
 p-(excited)
 d-They’ve formed a corporate entity. This is excellent. It creates a centralized target for financial pressure.

c-NIGEL
 d-Or it creates a tax nightmare for their accountants.

c-CLIVE
 d-You’re missing the strategic implications, Nigel. We can now infiltrate at the board level.

c-NIGEL
 d-They’re the board, Clive.

c-CLIVE
 d-Exactly! All we need is one director to see reason. The drummer seems... suggestable.

a-Nigel sighs, makes a note: “Monitor Apple Corps. Prepare infiltration scenarios.”

a-On the monitor, the Beatles walk away down the street, four young men who think they’ve just conquered the world.

a-They have no idea they’ve just built their own cage.

t-FADE OUT.
