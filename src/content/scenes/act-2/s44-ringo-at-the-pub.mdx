t-FADE IN:

s-INT. LONDON PUB - EVENING (EARLY 1968)

a-A cozy, noisy pub. Dark wood, dim lighting, the murmur of conversations and clinking glasses.

a-RINGO STARR sits alone at a small table in the corner, nursing a pint. He wears a simple jacket and looks completely at home. No one bothers him. Either they don’t recognize him, or they do and respect his privacy.

a-Two LOCALS at the bar talk loudly, not realizing he’s within earshot.

c-LOCAL #1
d-Did you hear? Lennon’s gone proper mental. Walking around in bedsheets with that Japanese bird.

c-LOCAL #2
d-McCartney’s gone posh. Thinks he’s a proper composer now. Saw him at some film premiere, looked like he was attending a funeral.

c-LOCAL #1
d-And the quiet one’s gone all mystical. Probably off in India somewhere, talking to statues.

a-Ringo takes a slow sip of his pint. A small smile plays on his lips.

c-LOCAL #2
d-Only one who’s still normal is the drummer. What’s his name?

c-LOCAL #1
d-No idea. Ring something.

a-Ringo’s smile widens slightly. He finishes his pint, stands, walks to the bar.

c-BARTENDER
d-Another, Mr. Starkey?

c-RINGO
d-Please, Bill. And one for yourself.

a-The bartender nods, pours. Ringo pays, leaves a generous tip.

c-RINGO
d-Carry on, then.

a-As he walks back to his table, he passes the two locals. They don’t even look up.

a-He sits down with his fresh pint. Looks around the room. People laughing, arguing, living their lives.

a-He pulls out a small notebook and a pen. Writes something simple:

a-“They’re still just boys. Scared, mostly. Same as everyone else.”

a-He closes the notebook. Finishes his pint. Stands to leave.

a-As he walks out, the bartender raises his glass in a small salute. Ringo nods back.

a-Outside, the London night is cool. He pulls up his collar and walks home, not like a Beatle, but like a man who knows exactly who he is.

t-FADE OUT.
