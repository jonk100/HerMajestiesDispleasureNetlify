t-FADE IN:

s-EXT. LEICESTER SQUARE  NIGHT (JANUARY 1967)

a-A modest FILM PREMIERE by West End standards. A small crowd, a few FLASHES, a half-hearted red carpet.

a-A MARQUEE reads:

a-"THE FAMILY WAY  MUSIC BY PAUL MCCARTNEY."

a-PAUL stands under it, tugging at a borrowed tuxedo. He looks up at his own name as if it belongs to someone else.

a-GEORGE MARTIN, ever composed, stands beside him, folding his gloves into one hand.

c-GEORGE MARTIN
p-(light)
 d-Well, there you are. Top of the bill. More or less.

c-PAUL
p-(trying not to grin)
 d-It's your orchestra, though. They'll blame you if it's rotten.

c-GEORGE MARTIN
 d-I shall simply tell them I was following orders.

a-They share a small, private laugh.

s-INT. CINEMA FOYER  LATER

a-The film has just finished. The audience buzzes politely.

a-POSTERS for respectable British dramas line the walls. This is not the screaming chaos of a Beatles gig. It is a world of murmurs and overcoats.

a-PAUL and GEORGE MARTIN stand with paper cups of wine. A SMALL KNOT of CRITICS and INDUSTRY TYPES hovers nearby.

c-CRITIC #1 (MISC REPORTER)
p-(to George Martin, but loudly enough for Paul)
 d-Extraordinary work, George. One quite... forgets it's that pop fellow.

a-Paul blinks.

c-GEORGE MARTIN
p-(pleasant)
 d-Paul wrote the themes. I merely dressed them.

c-CRITIC #1
p-(turning to Paul, appraising)
 d-Indeed. Astonishingly sophisticated, Mr. McCartney. A touch of... Noel Coward about it. Very mature.

a-"Sophisticated." "Mature." The words land carefully.

c-PAUL
p-(automatic)
 d-Oh, well... cheers. Its just a little tune, really.

c-CRITIC #2
p-(joining in)
 d-A tune you can whistle, though. Thats the thing. One hears so much... noise these days.

a-Paul hears an echo of Nigel the "piano tuner" without knowing why. "A tune you can whistle."

c-CRITIC #2 (CONT'D)
 d-This feels like proper music. Real... breeding.

a-Paul's smile tightens a fraction.

c-PAUL
p-(light)
 d-My dad'll like that bit.

a-The CRITICS chuckle politely and drift away, already composing their reviews in their heads.

s-CLOSE ON A NEWSPAPER DUMMY

a-A cultural editor's layout, glimpsed over a shoulder:

a-"YOUNG BEATLE SHOWS PROMISE OF A COMPOSER OF REAL BREEDING."

a-A blue pencil underlines "breeding" twice.

s-BACK TO PAUL AND GEORGE MARTIN

c-GEORGE MARTIN
p-(gentle)
 d-Take the compliment, Paul. You did well.

c-PAUL
p-(quietly)
 d-Yeah. It's just funny, isn't it? All this for twenty minutes of strings, and half the time with the band its, 'Turn that racket down.'

c-GEORGE MARTIN
 d-Different rooms, different ears.

a-Paul looks around at the FOYER: framed stills, tasteful carpet, a mild, approving crowd.

c-PAUL
 d-Feels a bit like... being let into the grown-ups' table.

c-GEORGE MARTIN
p-(wry)
 d-Be careful. It's terribly dull up here.

a-Paul laughs, but his eyes drift back to the marquee through the glass doors. "MUSIC BY PAUL MCCARTNEY."

s-INSERT  A TELEGRAM ON THE FOYER DESK

a-We catch only a fragment as an USHER sets it down among flowers:

a-"SUNDAY TIMES REVIEW CONFIRMED. LANGUAGE APPROVED.  F."

a-No one in the room notices it. We do.

s-BACK TO PAUL

a-He stares at his name flickering in the cold night outside.

c-PAUL
p-(soft, to himself)
 d-First time my name's up there without the others.

c-GEORGE MARTIN
p-(kind)
 d-It won't be the last.

a-Paul nods, not entirely reassured.

c-PAUL
 d-Yeah. Just dont want to lose what weve already got, you know?

a-He doesn't know yet that you can't keep both forever.
