t-FADE IN:

s-INT. KENWOOD - JOHN'S STUDY - NIGHT (LATE 1967)

a-A large, mock-Tudor room stuffed with the trophies of success: gold records, expensive stereos, shelves of books.

a-ON THE DESK: ashtrays, half-finished lyric sheets, a reel-to-reel machine, and a small stack of STRANGE OBJECTS.

a-A BROKEN TEACUP, painstakingly glued back together.

a-A neatly folded, EMPTY ENVELOPE labeled: "BREATHE."

a-A thin, hand-bound booklet titled: "INSTRUCTION PIECE." 

a-JOHN sits in semi-darkness, cigarette burning down between his fingers, glasses low on his nose as he reads.

c-YOKO (V.O.)
d-Imagine a line from the center of your chest to the sky.
d-Now imagine it is sound.

a-John turns a page. More cryptic text. More invitations.

c-YOKO (V.O.) (CONT'D)
d-Repair something broken. Keep it as a record of the time it was broken.

a-He looks at the glued cup. At his own nicotine-stained fingers.

c-JOHN
p-(to himself)
d-Right.

s-INT. KENWOOD - HALLWAY - CONTINUOUS

a-CYNTHIA moves quietly through the house in a dressing gown. It's late. The house is still, but not peaceful.

a-She pauses outside John's study, hearing the faint CLICK of the reel-to-reel, the scratch of his pen.

s-INT. STUDY - CONTINUOUS

a-Cynthia appears in the doorway, knocking softly on the frame.

c-CYNTHIA
d-John?

a-He doesn't look up immediately.

c-JOHN
d-Mm.

c-CYNTHIA
p-(gently)
d-It's after two.

a-He flips another page.

c-JOHN
d-Time's not real, Cyn. It's just clocks and taxmen.

a-She comes closer, taking in the debris of his mind: open books on religion and philosophy, scribbled margins, Yoko's odd artifacts.

c-CYNTHIA
d-You said we'd have an early night.

c-JOHN
p-(absently)
d-I said lots of things.

a-He finally looks at her. There is affection there, but at a distance. As if she's across a crowded room instead of three feet away.

c-CYNTHIA
p-(nodding at the objects)
d-More of her little presents?

a-He flinches almost imperceptibly.

c-JOHN
d-She's an artist. It's art.

c-CYNTHIA
d-It's our address.

a-Beat.

c-CYNTHIA (CONT'D)
d-And our phone number.

a-John stubs out his cigarette with unnecessary force.

c-JOHN
d-It's just ideas, Cyn. Little mind games.
p-(a faint, defensive smile)
d-Better than golf.

c-CYNTHIA
p-(trying not to sound hurt)
d-You have a son asleep upstairs who'd like a dad more than a mind game.

a-That lands.

a-John looks toward the ceiling. Toward JULIAN's room we don't see.

c-JOHN
p-(softly)
d-I know.

a-He reaches for another sheet from Yoko's booklet, as if against his will.

c-YOKO (V.O.)
d-Listen to the room when everyone has left. That is your true company.

a-The house HUMS: distant boiler, ticking clocks, the faint breath of suburban quiet.

c-JOHN
p-(bitter little laugh)
d-Company's rubbish.

c-CYNTHIA
d-You've got everything you ever said you wanted.

a-He looks around: the trophies, the house, the books.

c-JOHN
d-Yeah.
p-(beat)
d-That's the problem.

s-CLOSE ON THE BROKEN CUP

a-A hairline crack runs through the porcelain, the glue visible in the seams.

c-CYNTHIA (O.S.)
d-Who is she really, John?

s-BACK TO JOHN AND CYNTHIA

a-He searches for the neat answer. There isn't one.

c-JOHN
d-Someone who sends me things that make sense when everything else feels... plastic.

c-CYNTHIA
p-(hurt)
d-And I'm what?

c-JOHN
d-You're... home.

a-It's meant as comfort. It lands like a verdict.

a-Cynthia nods slowly. This is the sentence she didn't know she was afraid of.

c-CYNTHIA
d-Home's not supposed to feel like a cage, John.

a-She turns to go.

c-JOHN
p-(calling after her)
d-Neither is the world.

a-She pauses in the doorway but doesn't look back.

c-CYNTHIA
d-Then maybe stop bringing it all in here.

a-She exits.

a-John is alone again with the artifacts.

s-ON THE REEL-TO-REEL

a-It SPINS, tape hissing softly. Fragments of avant-garde noise, cut-ups, YOKO'S LAUGHTER faintly audible between leader tape.

c-YOKO (V.O.)
d-This is not a record. This is a question.

a-John leans back, eyes closed, letting the strange sounds wash over the Tudorbethan beams and tasteful wallpaper.

a-The house was sold to him as a dream. It now feels like the wrong costume.

c-JOHN
p-(to the ceiling)
d-What do you do when the cage is golden?

a-The only answer is the soft click of the tape reaching its end.
