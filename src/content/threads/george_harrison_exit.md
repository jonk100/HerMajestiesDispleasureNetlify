# George Harrison Exit Thread

**Overview**: This thread tracks George Harrison's journey from feeling creatively constrained and frustrated within the Beatles to his ultimate decision to leave the band. The arc spans Episode 4 entirely, opening with his first expressions of frustration at Twickenham and closing with his quiet departure in Scene 41.

**Key Moments**:
- **Scene 38.1**: First erosion - George feels "smaller, each interruption" as external production logistics repeatedly disrupt musical moments
- **Scene 39**: Multiple confrontations with Paul - George's creative contributions are systematically overlooked, leading to his famous statement "You don't annoy me anymore"
- **Scene 39.1**: Unacknowledged solution - George finds a chord progression that solves their arrangement problem, but receives no credit for it
- **Scene 40.1**: Brief return of joy - The "Get Back, Little Doggie" moment where old connection briefly returns before Paul reflexively reasserts control
- **Scene 41**: Quiet departure - George's final exit, putting down his guitar and walking out with "See you round the clubs"

**Thematic Significance**: This arc represents the cost of institutional success on artistic integrity. George achieves recognition that the Beatles' commercial success has come at the expense of the creative collaboration that made them special.
