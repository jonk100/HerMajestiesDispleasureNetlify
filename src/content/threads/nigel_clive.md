# Nigel and Clive

The series' central relationship, and its least romantic one. Nigel and Clive are not friends in any legible sense. They are colleagues in a specific institutional condition: they know the same things, they did the same things, and they were punished by the same institution for failing to do the one thing the institution wanted.

Their communication style is the thread's primary expression. Clive asks real questions. Nigel answers the surface question first, then the real one. The gap between the two answers is where the thread lives.

**Opening exchange (Ep. 1/2):** Nigel's measured assessment versus Clive's McLuhan misquote. The axis established: accuracy (Nigel) versus grandiose institutional thinking (Clive), with Clive's thinking ultimately prevailing not because it's correct but because it's what the institution wants.

**Operational peak:** The PEA E exchange after the concert. "Was that the plan?" "No." "Did it work?" "In the sense that he's still alive, yes." "And the banner?" "The banner will be somebody else's problem." Their last field exchange, compressed into five lines.

**Exile:** The identical desks. The civilian administrator who has no idea who they are. The shipping manifests. The wind.

**Close:** The radio. The look. The notebook. No dialogue after that. They look at each other. That's the end of them.
