# Nigel's Conscience

Nigel's conscience is the series' quietest and most consequential thread. It runs from his first clinical assessment in Episode 1 — "contextually unremarkable, no call to action" — through every beat he underlines, every minute he takes at a meeting he will need to forget, to the concert where he floods the lights instead of cutting them, to the Falklands where he crosses out a word that no one will ever read.

The thread doesn't announce itself. It lives in the gap between what Nigel observes and what he reports, between what he knows and what he says. The institution rewards anxiety over accuracy; his competence is a liability. His conscience is the part of him that keeps the accurate record even when the institution doesn't want one.

**Decisive moment:** The concert. Nigel at the lighting board. The plan is to cut the lights. He floods them. All of them. Whether this is deliberate is the question the series leaves open — but the notebook's crossed-out 'yet' answers it retroactively. He saved John with too much light.

**Close:** The radio announces John's death. December 1980. Ten years after the concert. The operation failed to kill him; the universe did not fail. Nigel opens the drawer. The notebook. He turns to the page. He crosses out the word. Slowly. One line. He closes the drawer. He looks at Clive. The wind. FADE TO BLACK.
