# Julian Thread

Julian Lennon appears once in the series — in Episode 2, in a surveillance observation Nigel makes through whatever field position allows him to see a father explaining guitar strings to a young son. John tells Julian the instrument is capable of "stories about love." Nigel writes it down. Below: "No action required."

That's the thread's seed. Everything else is what grows from it.

**Episode 2 — The observation:** Nigel sees John with Julian. He makes his correct assessment: the man explaining guitar strings to his child is not a threat requiring action. He writes it down. The notebook has the truth in it. The institution will eventually pressure him to revise it.

**Episode 6 (sc57) — Cynthia's letter:** Cynthia at the kitchen table, writing to Julian. Her crossed-out draft: "Your father was very complicated." Her kept line: "Your father made things that will last longer than either of us. That's not nothing." She posts the letter. The camera stays on the post box. The letter is in the system.

**Episode 6 (sc_falklands_1980) — The crossing-out:** Nigel opens the drawer. The notebook. The page. "No action required." / "Yet." He crosses out the word. He restores the original verdict. John is dead. Julian is somewhere in the world, having received the letter, holding the music.

The thread's logic: a child is the reason the correct choice was made. The correct choice was made. It didn't save his father. The choice was still correct. The thread closes on the page that started it.
