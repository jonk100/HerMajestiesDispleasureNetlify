# Glyn Johns

## Motivations

Glyn wants to capture great recordings and create a releasable album from the Get Back sessions. As a young engineer, working with the Beatles is a career-defining opportunity, but he's also pragmatic enough to recognize the challenges.

## Internal Conflict

Glyn is caught between his technical role (capturing good sound) and the reality that the band's dysfunction affects the recordings. He must work with what he gets while maintaining professional standards.

## Character Arc

**Act 2 (1969): The Overnight Worker** - Glyn spends the sessions capturing performances and then works overnight creating mixes. He's one of the few people who sees both the daytime chaos and the nighttime potential when he's alone with the tapes.

## Psychological Profile

**Core Traits:**

- Technically skilled and detail-oriented
- Pragmatic and solution-focused
- Young but professional
- Good at reading situations

**Strengths:**

- Excellent ear for sound and arrangements
- Works tirelessly (overnight mixing sessions)
- Suggests the rooftop concert idea with Michael Lindsay-Hogg
- Maintains professional standards under difficult conditions

## Key Relationships

### The Beatles (The Clients)

Glyn works with the Beatles in their most difficult period, capturing both their struggles and their moments of brilliance. He's professional enough to focus on the music rather than the drama.

### George Martin (The Mentor Figure)

Glyn works under George Martin's guidance, learning from the master producer while also operating with more independence than usual.

## Historical Timeline Context

**January 1969:** Glyn Johns engineers the Get Back sessions, recording at Twickenham and Apple Studios. He works overnight creating mixes while the band sleeps.

**January 25, 1969:** Along with Michael Lindsay-Hogg, Glyn suggests the rooftop concert after showing Ringo the view from Apple's roof.

**1969-1970:** Glyn creates multiple mixes of what would become the "Get Back/Let It Be" album, though his versions are eventually shelved in favor of Phil Spector's production.

**Typical Phrases:**

## Role in the Story

Glyn Johns represents the working professional trying to do his job amid chaos. His overnight mixing sessions show dedication to the craft even when the band's commitment wavers. His rooftop suggestion helps provide the climax the project needs.

Glyn serves as:
