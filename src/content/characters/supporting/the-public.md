## Motivations

The Public represents the complex relationship between The Beatles and their audience, embodying both the adoration that fuels their success and the expectations that limit their artistic freedom.

## Character Arc

**Act 1:** Adoring fans who elevate The Beatles to cultural icons  
**Act 2:** Growing expectations and cultural backlash as the band evolves  
**Act 3:** Mature audience that must reckon with the band's dissolution and legacy

## Relationships

- **The Beatles:** Object of worship and cultural fascination
- **Media:** Amplifier of public sentiment and cultural narrative
- **Institution:** Counterbalance to institutional control of culture
