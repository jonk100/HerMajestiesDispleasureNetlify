# Michael Lindsay-Hogg

## Motivations

Michael wants to make a great film. He's a professional director who sees the Beatles project as both an honor and an opportunity. As the sessions deteriorate, his motivation shifts from creating entertainment to salvaging something meaningful from the chaos - and ultimately to finding a proper ending.

## Internal Conflict

Michael is caught between his role as documentarian (capturing what's actually happening) and his need as a filmmaker (creating a coherent narrative with a satisfying conclusion). He watches the Beatles falling apart through his cameras but can't intervene without compromising the documentary nature of the film.

## Character Arc

**Act 2 (1969): The Desperate Director** - Michael begins enthusiastic about the TV special concept, becomes increasingly frustrated as the project loses shape and purpose, then finds redemption by proposing the rooftop concert. His suggestion saves both the sessions and his film.

## Psychological Profile

**Core Traits:**

- Professional and diplomatic
- Quick-thinking and solution-oriented
- Knows when to push and when to observe
- Understanding of group dynamics

**Strengths:**

- Can read a room and sense when intervention is needed
- Proposes the rooftop concert idea that becomes iconic
- Maintains professional distance while remaining empathetic

## Key Relationships

### The Beatles (The Subjects)

Michael needs to maintain a delicate balance - close enough to capture intimate moments, distant enough to let things unfold naturally. He watches them with a filmmaker's eye, seeing both the human drama and the historical significance.

### Paul McCartney (The Ally)

Paul is often Michael's main contact and ally, as he's the most invested in making the project work. Michael understands Paul's frustration about lacking a climax for the film.

### George Harrison (The Challenge)

George's departure creates Michael's biggest crisis - how do you make a Beatles documentary when there are only three Beatles?

## Historical Timeline Context

**January 1969:** Michael Lindsay-Hogg directs the Get Back documentary, capturing the Twickenham and Apple Studios sessions. His cameras record George's departure, the lunch conversations, and the evolution of the project.

**January 25, 1969:** Michael proposes the rooftop concert idea that becomes one of the Beatles' most iconic moments.

**January 30, 1969:** Michael directs the rooftop concert, creating the climax his film needed.

## Role in the Story

Michael Lindsay-Hogg represents the observer who becomes participant. His cameras document Operation Minuet's effects on the Beatles without him knowing about the conspiracy. His rooftop concert proposal provides an unintended solution - a way for the band to transcend their internal conflicts through public performance.

## Character Function

Michael serves as:
- **The Observer**: His cameras document everything, creating a record of the dissolution
- **The Problem Solver**: His rooftop proposal saves the project
- **The Audience Surrogate**: He asks the questions viewers would ask
- **The Professional**: He represents the external world's expectations and needs
