Below is the **complete analysis**, incorporating the prior evaluation and expanding it with a **clean chronological reconstruction of the mid-1966 → 1969 sequence** so you can see how the fixes interact structurally.

The goal is to keep three things aligned:

#### 1. Scene 35 — INDICA GALLERY (John meets Yoko)

##### Problem

Scene 35 currently appears **after scene 34 (Bangor – August 27, 1967)**.

That places John meeting Yoko **more than a year after** he is already deeply involved with her in later Kenwood scenes.

The Kenwood scenes (35.1 and 35.2) reference Yoko as an **established presence**, which makes the current order logically impossible.

##### Key detail

Scene 26 is **not the same gallery event**.

* **Scene 26:** Indica Gallery encounter with Klaus/Clive
* **Scene 35:** John meets Yoko

This means moving scene 35 near scene 26 **does not create duplication**.

##### Correct placement

The best chronological position is:

```
26 – Indica Gallery (Klaus / Clive encounter)
27 – existing scene
35 – John meets Yoko
28 – following events
```

This keeps the **1966 London art-scene environment intact**.

##### Conclusion

Move **Scene 35 → after scene 27**.

---

#### 2. Scene 36.1 — Candlestick Park (August 29, 1966)

##### Problem

This scene is currently placed **after scene 36 (July 1968)**.

That places the Beatles’ **final concert two years late**, which breaks a major cause-and-effect chain.

Candlestick Park represents:

* the **last live performance**
* the **end of touring**
* the transition into the **studio-only era**

##### Correct narrative chain

The proper structural flow should be:

```
India / spiritual searching
Brian’s office (Aug 1966)
Candlestick Park – final concert
→ band abandons touring
→ studio experimentation begins
```

##### Correct placement

```
32 – India
32.1 – Brian’s office (Aug 1966)
36.1 – Candlestick Park (Aug 29, 1966)
33 – onward
```

##### Conclusion

Move **36.1 → immediately after 32.1**.

#### 4. Scene 38.5 — MI5 surveillance (“Revolution 9”)

##### Problem

That makes it appear as though the MI5 operatives are listening to “Revolution 9” **during the Get Back rehearsals**, which is historically wrong.

##### Two possible approaches

Move the scene near the late-1968 material.


###### Option B — Dramatic archival framing

##### Recommendation

Move **38.5 near scene 36.3**, unless the script explicitly frames it as archival playback.
