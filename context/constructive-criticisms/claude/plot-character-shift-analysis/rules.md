# SCENE-BY-SCENE CHECKLIST GUIDE

## How to Use This Framework

For each scene in your screenplay, ask these three questions:

### **1. PLOT (Y/N)**
**Does this scene move the story forward with new information or a decision that creates consequences?**

**YES means:**
- A character learns something they didn't know
- A character makes a decision that forces the next scene to happen
- An event occurs that changes the situation
- Information is revealed that shifts the trajectory

**NO means:**
- The scene just reminds us of things we already know
- It's atmosphere or mood without new story information
- Characters discuss the situation without changing it

**Example from your script:**
- **Episode 1, MBE Bathroom scene** = PLOT YES (They smoke cannabis in the Queen's bathroom - this is the inciting incident that triggers Philip's reaction)
- **Episode 1, Brian's celebration** = PLOT NO (We already know they got MBEs; this scene just shows them celebrating)

---

### **2. CHARACTER (Y/N)**
**Does this scene deepen or reveal something essential about who these people are?**

**YES means:**
- We learn a character's true motivation
- We see a character change their mind about something important
- A relationship between characters fundamentally shifts
- A character reveals vulnerability, hypocrisy, or hidden desire

**NO means:**
- Characters behave exactly as we'd expect with no new insight
- The scene shows us what we already know about them
- It's just "on brand" behavior without depth

**Example from your script:**
- **Episode 4, John/Paul flowerpot conversation** = CHARACTER YES (We see the specific power dynamic and creative tension between them laid bare)
- **Episode 2, Beatles rehearsal with tea service** = CHARACTER NO (Nigel serving tea doesn't reveal anything new about The Beatles)

---

### **3. POWER SHIFT (Y/N)**
**Does someone gain or lose leverage, control, or status in this scene?**

**YES means:**
- Character A starts the scene in control, Character B ends in control
- A character gains knowledge/evidence/authority that changes the balance
- A character loses credibility, leverage, or agency
- The status quo is disrupted

**NO means:**
- The power dynamic is exactly the same at the end as the beginning
- Everyone maintains their established positions
- No one wins or loses anything

**Example from your script:**
- **Episode 1, Philip watching TV** = POWER SHIFT YES (He goes from smug control to recognizing a threat)
- **Episode 2, Nigel in surveillance van** = POWER SHIFT NO (He's still just observing; nothing changes)

---

## THE RULE

**Every scene should score at least 2 out of 3.**

If a scene only does ONE thing:
- **Only PLOT**: Cut it or fold the information into another scene
- **Only CHARACTER**: Beautiful but indulgent - add stakes or consequences
- **Only POWER SHIFT**: Cool moment but needs grounding in story or character

If a scene does NONE of them:
- **Cut it immediately.** It's dead weight.


# QUICK REFERENCE QUESTIONS

When you're stuck on whether something counts:

**PLOT:**
- "If I cut this scene, would the audience be confused about what happens next?"
- "Does a character learn or decide something that forces the next scene?"

**CHARACTER:**
- "Do I understand this person better after this scene?"
- "Would I describe them differently to a friend after watching this?"

**POWER:**
- "Who's winning at the start vs. the end?"
- "Has anyone's leverage/status/control changed?"
