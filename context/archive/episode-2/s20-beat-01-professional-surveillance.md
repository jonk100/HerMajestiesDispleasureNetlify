Nigel sits alone in the surveillance van. Rain on the roof, smell of damp wool and instant coffee. The routine of intelligence work - headphones on, listening through static for nothing in particular.

Professional detachment. The kettle boiling, distant laughter - all just ambient noise in the mission. Nigel is the institution's ears, filtering the world for threats.

He listens for subversion, for ideology, for the memetic warfare Clive theorizes. He expects to hear the cultural polluter at work.

The setup is complete - professional observer monitoring suspected cultural threat through the cold technology of surveillance.
