Sir Derek's head snaps toward Nigel. For moment, silence. Then face breaks into something that might be smile if smiles could cause frostbite.

"Well, well. The comedian speaks. Tell me, Finch, do you find this funny?" Pointer sweeps across album cover. "Do you find the systematic dismantling of everything this country stands for amusing? The Beatles aren't just making music anymore! They're creating a new reality! A reality where Marx plays bass and Crowley handles percussion!"

He walks to window, looks out at perfectly manicured palace gardens. "The Queen asked me this morning if I thought 'Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds' was about her granddaughter. I had to lie. I had to say it was about a drawing. We both know it's about LSD. We both know it's about turning the next generation into psychedelic zombies who will hand over the keys to the kingdom for a sitar solo."

He turns back, pointer aimed directly between Nigel's eyes. "So here's what's going to happen. Operation Minuet is escalating. We're moving from psychological warfare to cultural counter-insurgency. You two will not just observe. You will intervene. You will neutralize. You will understand?"
