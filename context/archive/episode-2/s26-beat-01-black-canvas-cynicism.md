Stark white gallery filled with aggressively incomprehensible art. Rusted bicycle wheel, neat pile of bricks labeled. Young, stoned crowd wearing sunglasses indoors.

John wanders through, slightly high, deeply unimpressed. Stops in front of large canvas painted entirely black. Stares at it for long beat.

"Well. It's dark, isn't it?"

Clive slides into frame as "Klaus" - black turtleneck, beret, tinted spectacles. Parody of European intellectual assembled from secondhand rumors.

"I am Klaus. Klaus... von... Klaus. Critic. Visionary. I have followed your work, Herr Lennon."

"Have you? Which bit? The 'Yeah Yeah Yeah' bit or the bit where I shake my head?"

John's cynicism about avant-garde established, while Klaus's ridiculous identity immediately creates absurd dynamic.
