Reaches for pencil, scribbles on scrap: \"RESPECTABLE MOUTH.\"\"ORDINARY.\"NOT ME." Underlines last one so hard pencil snaps.

He stands, paces. Picks up newspaper headline: "LENNON QUESTIONS CHRISTIANITY." Smiles. "They never ask the right audience."

Opens drawer. Inside: thin sheaf of forms - names and addresses in neat columns. Not official forms. Something he's made to look official.

Flips to page headed: "PERSONAE."

Under it: \"MRS. GLADYS PEMBERTON — Birmingham, Alabama (churchgoing, widow)\"\"REV. HAROLD K. SLOANE — Jackson, Mississippi (youth sermon tone)\"MR. WILLIAM D. TANNER — Dallas, Texas (veteran, law-and-order)"

He reads them like characters in a play. The cast assembled for manufactured outrage.
