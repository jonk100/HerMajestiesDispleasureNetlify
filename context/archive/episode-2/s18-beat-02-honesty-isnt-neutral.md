Paul stops pacing, trying to translate John into consequences. "Yeah, but facts upset people."

John's response reveals his core philosophy: "Only if they were using them as furniture. And half of them are."

Paul escalates to the fundamental insight: "You don't get how it sounds." Then the crucial formulation: "Honest isn't neutral. You say a thing and it stops being yours."

This is the heart of the conflict. John sees himself as thinker; Paul understands that public figures don't own their own words. "They'll do it for you. They'll take the best bit, the worst bit, the funniest bit—and that's you, forever."
