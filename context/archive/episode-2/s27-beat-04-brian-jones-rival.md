"But how do you even begin to learn that? It seems… impossibly complex."

Nigel feigns thoughtful pause, accessing cultural trivia. "It's interesting you should ask. There's another young man, a customer. From that group, The Rolling Stones. The blond one." Dismissive wave. "A bit more... frantic, their music. But he is a very serious student."

This piques George's interest intensely. A peer. A rival, even.

"Brian Jones?"

"That's the one. He tells me the only way to truly understand the music is to go to the master himself. He's been taking lessons. From Mr. Shankar."

The pieces click into place for George. Not just practice; apprenticeship. A lineage. The spiritual journey becomes competitive race.
