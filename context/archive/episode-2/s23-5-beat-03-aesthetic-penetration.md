Clive reaches into messenger bag, pulls out sketchbook with crudely drawn abstract composition.

"I am here studying the... how you say... cultural penetration of popular music into the avant-garde space. The Beatles, for example."

"The Beatles? They're not avant-garde. They're pop."

"Exactly! That is what makes them fascinating! The way their... capitalist accessibility masks their deeper revolutionary potential. Like Warhol, but with more... melody."

Across room, Paul McCartney and Jane Asher walk past. Clive's eyes widen behind glasses.

"In fact, I am developing what I call 'aesthetic penetration protocols' to understand how their influence might... reshape the cultural landscape."

"Aesthetic penetration protocols? That sounds like something else entirely."

"No, no! It is... artistic methodology. Very sophisticated. I am trying to determine if their album covers might benefit from more... existential weight."
