Paul's music room - tasteful, ordered, deliberate contrast to John's chaos. Paul sits on sofa, strumming acoustic guitar. He plays beautiful melodic fragment of "Here, There and Everywhere" - soft, sweet, effortless.

Nigel Finch enters in blue coveralls embroidered "B&H PIANO SERVICES." He strikes piano key. PING. Tightens string. PING.

Paul stops playing. "Sounding better?"

"Getting there, Mr. McCartney. Getting there. Lovely tune that."

Paul brightens despite himself. "You think? Bit soft, maybe?"

"Nah. That's what people want, isn't it? A nice tune. Something you can whistle to while the kettle boils."

The setup complete. Paul's melodic identity affirmed, Nigel's working-class authenticity established. The psychological operation begins.
