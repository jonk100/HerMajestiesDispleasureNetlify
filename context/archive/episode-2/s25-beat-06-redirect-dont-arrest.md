"So what sort of threat is that?" Clive asks, confused.

Nigel stands, pushing briefcase toward Clive. He places understandingly exact change on table.

"The sort you don't arrest." Matter-of-fact. "The sort you redirect."

He starts to walk away, but stops and turns to Clive. "Let's never do this again."

He walks away. Café remains unchanged.

Clive opens device, connects headphones, twiddles with buttons. No luck. He puts it away and stares through window. Eyes drift to American clipping beneath Nigel's hand.

"If there's no voice… you manufacture one."

Nigel doesn't react. He simply stirs his tea. Clive's determination hardens into something practical.

The institutional man has understood. Cultural threats aren't arrested; they're redirected. And if there's no voice to redirect, you manufacture one.
