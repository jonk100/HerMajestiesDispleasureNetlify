Percy turns and disappears into the aisles. After rummaging, he returns, placing a heavy metal box on the counter with loud THUD. It looks like a car battery with a large cone attached.

"Item 47-B. Parabolic Listening Device. German. 1953. Very reliable."

The technological absurdity revealed. Clive's dreams of state-of-the-art surveillance equipment meet 1953 German engineering. Thirteen years of technological progress rendered irrelevant by institutional inventory.

"Right. And... how does one conceal that? In a bread van?" Clive asks, completely deflated.

Nigel provides the institutional answer: "We're not concealing it. We're placing it at an angle and hoping no one asks."

The gap between operational need and institutional reality becomes a chasm.
