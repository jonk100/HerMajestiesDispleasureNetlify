"That's not what concerns me," Clive shakes head.

"Oh?"

"There was no center. No voice. No instruction. Just… diffusion."

"That's always been the case with popular culture."

"No. Popular culture amplifies authority. This dissolves it."

Nigel considers this, genuinely. "They don't issue commands."

"Exactly. They don't replace authority — they make it irrelevant."

Nigel stirs tea again, slower now. "I'd argue the opposite. They become it. Accidentally."

"You don't think they know what they're doing?"

"I think it doesn't matter whether they do." Nigel glances at clipping. "It matters what other people decide they meant."

The philosophical breakthrough. Clive's theory finally makes sense to Nigel. The Beatles aren't issuing commands; they're creating vacuum where authority used to be.
