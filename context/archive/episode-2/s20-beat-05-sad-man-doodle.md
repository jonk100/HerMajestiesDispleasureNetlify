Nigel checks his watch again, then looks down at the notebook. He's doodled a picture of a sad man in a suit in the bottom corner of the page. The suit has a tie. The tie has been shaded in.

The unconscious confession. The sad man in the suit is not Lennon. It's Nigel himself - the institutional man trapped by professional detachment, haunted by the human moments he's supposed to monitor but not feel.

He quickly scribbles it out as if it might be discoverable in court. The act of a man who understands evidence, who knows that even unconscious drawings can be used against you.

The professional observer has become the observed. The institutional man has been exposed to himself.
