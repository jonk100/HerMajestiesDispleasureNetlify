Through the static, something clear emerges. Not ideology, not subversion, but domestic intimacy:

"Julian! Be careful with that, son. That's daddy's guitar."

A small child's giggle. "Why?"

"Because it's got stories in it. And we don't want the stories to fall out."

Nigel freezes. Pen stops tapping. This is not the cultural polluter Clive described. This is a father explaining the magic of music to his five-year-old son.

"What stories?"

"Stories about love. And about being sad sometimes. And about..."

John's voice fades as he moves away from microphone. "Come on, let's let mummy have a go. You can help me play the big chords."

The professional observer has encountered the unfileable - tenderness.
