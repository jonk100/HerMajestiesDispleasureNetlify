# Episode 1 Sequencing

The episode breaks naturally into four sequences, each with its own dramatic thrust and tonal identity.

## Sequence 1: The Investiture (Scenes 1–5)
The bathroom, the ceremony, the corridor, the press scrum. This is the inciting incident — the powder keg. Everything flows from John's casual disrespect and Philip's decision to make it mean something. It ends with the two men separated and heading toward collision.

## Sequence 2: The Reaction & The Plan (Scenes 6–9)
Philip watches the broadcast, calls Mountbatten, the palace meeting happens, and Operation Minuet is born in the MI5 briefing room. This is the "antagonist mobilizes" sequence. It ends with Nigel underlining "encourage sitar studies?" — the comedy of the establishment's absurd overreach is fully established here.

## Sequence 3: Rubber Soul & The Surveillance Farce (Scenes 10–11)
Abbey Road at night through the album's success montage. This is the thematic heart — the band naturally evolving into something bigger while Nigel and Clive catastrophically misread everything. The sequence ends with Nigel dwarfed by the crowd outside the record shop. The establishment's plan is already losing before it's begun.

## Sequence 4: The Three Portraits & The Fuse (Scenes 12–16)
Paul with the orchestra, John at Weybridge with his books, the Maureen Cleave interview setup, and finally the interview itself. These three parallel portraits — Paul reaching toward legitimacy, John reaching toward meaning, Clive reaching toward paranoid delusion — build to the episode's final image: John's words recorded on the page, innocent in the moment, loaded with future consequence.

## Notes

The dramatic architecture is essentially a slow-burning irony: the more genuinely the Beatles grow, the more threatening they appear to people looking for threats.
