# **OPERATION MINUET**

**A 6-Part Limited Series**

## **I. THE LOGLINE**

When The Beatles are awarded MBEs in 1965, Prince Philip responds not with pride but by quietly unleashing MI5 to rein them in. Caught between royal paranoia and John Lennon's growing rebellion, two hapless intelligence officers oversee a campaign that exposes how petty grudges, bad paperwork, and institutional absurdity can reshape history.

## **II. THE STORY OVERVIEW**

The series spans from 1965 to 1970, tracking the covert, multi-year campaign orchestrated by the British Crown to neutralize The Beatles as a "cultural threat". What begins as a petty response to a perceived insult at Buckingham Palace evolves into a sprawling operation involving psychological warfare, orchestrated scandals, and eventually, a failed assassination attempt.

The narrative explores the "miserable symmetry" of power—where the rigid machinery of the state attempts to crush the chaotic influence of art, only to find itself transformed by the encounter.

## **III. TONE & STYLE**

* **Tone**: Dark, cynical, and deeply satirical. It focuses on the absurdity of royal institutions and the petty human motivations behind grand historical events.
* **Visual Language**: A clash of two distinct worlds:
* **The Royal/MI5 World**: Rigid symmetry, desaturated palettes (greys, muted blues), and dusty, sterile production design. Framing is deadpan and wide, emphasizing institutional absurdity.
* **The Beatles' World**: Chaotic, warm, and organic with handheld or fluid camera movements. Spaces are cluttered with life and art, becoming progressively more counter-culture as the series moves toward 1970.


* **Structural Comps**: The bureaucratic melancholy of *Patriot*, the dysfunctional family dynamics of *The Royal Tenenbaums*, and the sharp institutional satire of *The Death of Stalin*.

## **IV. CORE THEMES**

* **Power vs. Art**: The conflict between inherited state power and earned creative influence.
* **The Absurdity of Bureaucracy**: How history is shaped not by grand design, but by middle-manager incompetence and flawed paperwork.
* **Myth vs. Reality**: The gap between the public icons of the Monarchy/Beatles and their private, dysfunctional realities.

## **V. KEY CHARACTERS**

### **The Operatives**

* **Nigel Finch (48-53)**: A world-weary MI5 agent who just wants to reach his pension. Over the five-year operation, his conscience evolves, transforming him from Philip's tool into a quiet saboteur of the mission.
* **Clive Barrow (41-46)**: An eccentric, delusional agent who views "Operation Minuet" as his artistic masterpiece. He creates unnecessarily complex solutions and infiltrates the band's circle using his "Klaus" persona.

### **The Crown**

* **Prince Philip (44-49)**: The primary antagonist. Offended by John Lennon's "killing people" comment, his wounded pride drives a five-year obsession that escalates from psychological warfare to attempted assassination.
* **Lord Mountbatten (65-70)**: Philip's strategic mentor who architects the operation using Cold War tactical mindsets to neutralize a pop group's cultural influence.

### **The Beatles**

* **John Lennon (25-30)**: The rebellious catalyst whose casual irreverence triggers the Crown's vendetta. His authenticity is both his greatest strength and his primary vulnerability.
* **Paul McCartney (23-28)**: The diplomatic workhorse trying to maintain group cohesion while unknowingly being targeted by fake intelligence meant to isolate him from the band.
* **George Harrison (22-27)**: The spiritual seeker whose creative frustrations make him an unwitting target for MI5's "sitar study" manipulations.
* **Ringo Starr (25-30)**: The "unflappable heart" whose simple decency makes him accidentally immune to psychological warfare.

## **VI. EPISODE OUTLINE**

* **Episode 1: The Establishment's Gambit (Scenes 1-16)**: After the Beatles smoke a joint in the Buckingham Palace bathroom, John Lennon makes a flippant remark about military service that triggers Prince Philip's fury. Operation Minuet is born.
* **Episode 2: The Infiltration (Scenes 17-31)**: Nigel and Clive begin their "nudges," including amplifying the "More popular than Jesus" controversy and using Clive's "Klaus" persona to radicalize John's artistic leanings.
* **Episode 3: The Center Cannot Hold (Scenes 32-49)**: The death of manager Brian Epstein removes the band's primary protector, an event the Crown views as a "tactical advantage".
* **Episode 4: The Sound of Chaos (Scenes 50-60)**: As the band fractures during the *White Album* sessions, the agents become lost in their own paranoia, trying to find Soviet coordinates in avant-garde recordings.
* **Episode 5: The Long and Winding Road (Scenes 61-71)**: The grueling *Get Back* sessions. Realizing psychological warfare isn't enough, Philip demands "Operation Elegy"—a final, lethal solution.
* **Episode 6: The Ultimate Cosmic Joke (Scenes 72-87)**: The Rooftop Concert and the final dissolution of the band. In a 1980 epilogue, Nigel and Clive realize the absurdity of their war when a "random madman" achieves what the entire state could not.

## **VII. THE EPILOGUE**

The series concludes with Nigel and Clive, now aged and forgotten in a Falklands office, listening to the news of Lennon's death. It ends on the "ultimate, cosmic joke": that their five-year secret war was fought and lost for absolutely nothing.
