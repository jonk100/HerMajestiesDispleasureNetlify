# Historical Context

## Timeline

### 1957

* **July 6** – John Lennon meets Paul McCartney at the Woolton Parish Church garden fête in Liverpool.
* Lennon’s band *The Quarrymen* later evolves into *The Beatles* as George Harrison and Ringo Starr eventually join.

### 1960

* The Beatles begin playing extended gigs in Hamburg, Germany.
* They hone their sound and image through relentless live performance.

### 1962

* The Beatles sign with EMI Records.
* **October 5** – Release of first single, “Love Me Do.”

### 1963

* Beatlemania erupts in the UK following the success of singles like “Please Please Me” and “She Loves You.”
* Media attention intensifies; screaming fans become a hallmark of Beatles concerts.

### 1964

* **February** – The Beatles arrive in the U.S. and appear on *The Ed Sullivan Show* to record-breaking viewership.
* Their global fame is cemented almost overnight.
* Release of *A Hard Day’s Night* film and album.

### 1965

* **October 26** – The Beatles are appointed **Members of the Order of the British Empire (MBE)** by Queen Elizabeth II.
* This move is controversial among traditionalists; some prior recipients return their honors in protest.

### 1966

* **March** – John Lennon comments in an interview that The Beatles are "more popular than Jesus,” igniting outrage in the U.S.
* **August 29** – The Beatles play their final official concert at Candlestick Park, San Francisco.
* The band retreats from live performance and becomes a studio-focused group.

### 1967

* **June 1** – Release of *Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band*, widely considered a masterpiece.
* **August 27** – Death of manager Brian Epstein, destabilizing the band’s leadership.

### 1968

* The Beatles launch Apple Corps, a multimedia company that quickly becomes chaotic and financially unstable.
* **November 22** – Release of the *White Album*, showcasing internal creative tensions.
* John begins an increasingly public relationship with **Yoko Ono** and becomes politically outspoken.

### 1969

* **March** – John and Yoko stage their first *Bed-In for Peace* in Amsterdam.
* **April** – John officially changes his middle name from Winston to Ono.
* **November 25** – John returns his **MBE medal**, with a note:

  > “Your Majesty, I am returning this MBE as a protest against Britain’s involvement in the Nigeria-Biafra thing, against our support of America in Vietnam and against Cold Turkey slipping down the charts.”
* This act is quietly mocked or condemned by the establishment, particularly among royal and political elites.
* Release of *Abbey Road* marks the end of an era.

### 1970

* **April 10** – Paul McCartney publicly announces The Beatles have broken up.
* The breakup is messy, litigious, and deeply emotional.

### 1971–1980

* John lives mostly in New York with Yoko Ono.
* Becomes increasingly involved in anti-war activism, under surveillance by the FBI and Nixon administration.
* Releases solo work including *Imagine*, *Mind Games*, *Walls and Bridges*, and *Double Fantasy*.
* He remains a pop icon and polarizing figure.

### 1980

* **December 8** – John Lennon is shot and killed by Mark David Chapman outside The Dakota apartment building in New York City.
* Though the assassination is carried out by a disturbed fan, conspiracy theories abound.
